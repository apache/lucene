'''Daniël (Daan) van Golden''' (4 February 1936 – 10 January 2017) was a Dutch artist, who has been active as painter, photographer, collagist, installation artist, wall painter and graphic artist.<ref name="RKD">[https://rkd.nl/nl/explore/artists/32469 Daan van Golden; man / Nederlands; schilder, fotograaf, collagist, installatiekunstenaar, wandschilder, graficus] at rkd.nl, 2015.</ref> He is known for his meticulous paintings of motives and details of everyday life and every day images.<ref>Bianca Stigter, "[https://www.nrc.nl/nieuws/2017/01/10/kunstenaar-daan-van-golden-overleden-rotterdamse-pop-art-6149725-a1540566 Pop-art kunstenaar Daan van Golden overleden], ''NRC Handelsblad,'' 10 januari 2017. Accessed 25.10.2018.</ref><ref>Sacha Bronwasser. "Nederland verliest met Daan van Golden een van zijn grootste kunstenaars," ''Volkskrant,'' 10.01.2017.</ref><ref>Kees Keijer, "[https://www.parool.nl/amsterdam/daan-van-golden-1936-2017-was-meester-van-ingetogen-alledaagsheid~a4446787/ Daan van Golden (1936-2017) was meester van ingetogen allerdaagse]," ''Het Parool,'' 11.10.2017.</ref>

== Biography ==
=== Youth, education and early career ===
Born on [[Katendrecht]] in Rotterdam in 1936, Van Golden was initially trained as [[fitter (occupation)|fitter]] at a technical school from 1948 to 1950. On Sundays he received painting lessons from a Jesuit who was attached to the school. 

From 1954 to 1959 he attended the evening courses at the Rotterdam Academy of Visual Arts (now [[Willem de Kooning Academy]]) at the painting department, where he also took classes in graphic art and design.<ref name="RKD"/>

Van Golden started working as independent artist in [[Schiedam]] in 1961.<ref name="RKD"/>  Initially he was concerned with [[action painting]] and painted ferocious work with thickly applied black paint. 

From 1963 to 1965 he made a two and a half year trip to [[Japan]], which turned his work around.<ref name="HVV 1965">"[http://resolver.kb.nl/resolve?urn=ddd:010955440:mpeg21:p018 Daan van Golden (terug van 3 jaar Japan): „In Nederland bekijkt men een kunstenaar als clown"]." ''Het vrĳe volk : democratisch-socialistisch dagblad,'' Rotterdam, 1965/04/28, p. 18.</ref><ref name="PB 1965">"Van Golden blijkt een opmerkelijk vernieuwer". "Het vrĳe volk : democratisch-socialistisch dagblad". Rotterdam, 1965/05/21, p. 15.</ref> There he came into contact with meditation and he realized that the human mind can find peace in watching simple form carefully. These simple shapes could simply be found in the everyday life. 

=== Later career ===
[[File:De Verwondering, 3823 Amersfoort, Netherlands - panoramio (3).jpg|thumb|Menhir sculpture, Amersfoort 1995.]]
Upon returning to the Netherlands Daan van Golden started painting copies of brown packing paper to find peace of mind. This painting was the beginning of a series of works, created between 1963 and 1975, in which simple objects such as handkerchiefs, dishcloths, wrapping paper, tablecloths and other decorative material were repainted down to the smallest details.

After a period of painting diamond-shaped motifs, in a new period Van Golden dealt with copying public [[newspaper]] photographs, posters and advertisements. Hereby he made use of photography and screen printing technique. Later in the 1980s Van Golden became inspired by the drippings paintings of [[Jackson Pollock]], which inspired him to create a series of paintings in which the silhouettes of paint drips minutely created strange characters.

In the early 1990s Golden turned to depicting silhouettes of photos and portraits. He copied painted profiles of Mozart. These silhouette paintings he mostly made in several versions. The personal style of the artist makes each painting unique, despite the compositional similarities with other paintings.

In 1987 Van Golden and [[Paul Beckman (artist)|Paul Beckman]] were awarded the [[Hendrik Chabot Award]].<ref>

== Work ==
Van Golden was influenced by pop art, abstract expressionism, minimalism and conceptual art. His paintings are realistic representations of subjects from mass production, but can be considered as abstract art as well. In his paintings of everyday objects, Golden deliberately chose to omit light and shade so that only the one-dimensional decorative motifs remained. The result was a selection of abstract paintings that consisted only of straight lines and flat color planes.

At the time of his death at Schiedam in early 2017 van Golden was still active. Due to a meticulous way of working, he made an average of only 3 to 4 pieces per year. He exhibited little and was regarded as a mysterious artist.

== Reception ==
The pioneering work of Van Golden of painting [[dishcloth]] motives was acknowledged in 1972, when his work was used as inspiration for poster of the exhibition "Illusion" in the [[Stedelijk Museum Schiedam]]. Harry Willemsen stated, that the pictorial approach of reality of Van Golden had become quite present in those days.<ref>Harry Willemsen, "[http://resolver.kb.nl/resolve?urn=ddd:011236516:mpeg21:p005 Artistiek schildersbedrog in de loop der eeuwen]," ''De tĳd : dagblad voor Nederland,'' Amsterdam, 1973/08/16, p. 5.</ref> In an 1986 newspaper article Jan Donia after a analyses of his work acknowledged, that of Van Golden held a unique position in the field of contemporary art. His work was a 'mysterie of imagination'.<ref>Jan Donia, "[http://resolver.kb.nl/resolve?urn=ddd:010948741:mpeg21:p013 VAN GOLDEN; het raadsel van de verbeelding]," ''Het vrĳe volk : democratisch-socialistisch dagblad,'' Rotterdam, 1986/03/13, p. 13.</ref>
