[[File:Uranocene-2D-skeletal.png|thumb|right|100px|The chemical structure of [[uranocene]] includes a uranium atom sandwiched between two cyclooctatetraenyl groups]]
An '''actinocene''' is a type of [[organoactinide]] [[metallocene]] compound that contains an [[Chemical element|element]] from the [[actinide]] series. The typical structure is a [[sandwich compound]], with two [[cyclooctatetraene|cyclooctatetraenyl]] di[[anion]]s (cot, which is {{chem|C|8|H|8|2−}}) bound to an actinide-metal center (An) in the [[oxidation state]] IV, with the resulting general formula An(C<sub>8</sub>H<sub>8</sub>)<sub>2</sub><ref>{{cite journal |last= Minasian |first= Stefan G. |first2= Jason M. |last2=Keith |title= New evidence for 5f covalency in actinocenes determined from carbon K-edge XAS and electronic structure theory |year= 2014 |journal= [[Chemical Science (journal)|Chem. Sci.]] |volume= 5 |issue= 1 |pages= 351-359 |doi= 10.1039/C3SC52030G|url= https://zenodo.org/record/1230028/files/article.pdf }}</ref>

The most studied actinocene is [[uranocene]], U(C<sub>8</sub>H<sub>8</sub>)<sub>2</sub>.<ref name="Seyferth">{{cite journal |last= Seyferth |first= D. |title= Uranocene. The First Member of a New Class of Organometallic Derivatives of the f Elements |year= 2004 |journal= [[Organometallics]] |volume= 23 |issue= 15 |pages= 3562–3583 |doi= 10.1021/om0400705|doi-access= free }}</ref>

The actinide-cyclooctatetraenyl [[chemical bond|bonding]] was shown by [[computational chemistry]] to be mainly due to mixing of actinide 6d orbitals into ligand π-[[molecular orbitals|orbitals]] and therefore donation of electronic charge to the actinide, with a smaller such interaction involving the actinide 5f orbitals. <ref>{{cite journal |last= Kerridge |first= Andrew |title= f-Orbital covalency in the actinocenes (An = Th–Cm): multiconfigurational studies and topological analysis |year= 2014 |journal= [[RSC Advances]] |volume= 4 |issue=  |pages= 12078-12086 |doi= 10.1039/C3RA47088A|url= http://discovery.ucl.ac.uk/1426903/1/c3ra47088a.pdf }}</ref>

Other actinocenes include protactinocene (Pa(C<sub>8</sub>H<sub>8</sub>)<sub>2</sub>), thorocene (Th(C<sub>8</sub>H<sub>8</sub>)<sub>2</sub>), [[neptunocene]] (Np(C<sub>8</sub>H<sub>8</sub>)<sub>2</sub>), and plutonocene (Pu(C<sub>8</sub>H<sub>8</sub>)<sub>2</sub>).

Sandwiched M(C<sub>8</sub>H<sub>8</sub>)<sub>2</sub> compounds (actinocenes and [[lanthanocene]]s) exist for M = [[neodymium|Nd]], [[terbium|Tb]], [[plutonium|Pu]], [[protactinium|Pa]], [[neptunium|Np]], [[thorium|Th]], [[Uranium|U]] and [[ytterbium|Yb]].<ref name="Seyferth" />
