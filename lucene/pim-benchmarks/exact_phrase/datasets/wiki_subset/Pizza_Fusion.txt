'''Pizza Fusion''' is a [[Deerfield Beach, Florida|Deerfield Beach]], [[Florida]]-based<ref name="breaking">{{cite web  | title = Breaking New Ground| work = QSR Magazine |last=Scott |first=Sherri Daye |date=March 2008| url = http://www.qsrmagazine.com/articles/interview/113/pizzafusion_part1-1.phtml}}</ref> [[pizza]] [[restaurant chain]]. Using mostly [[organic food|organic]] ingredients and emphasizing [[green building]] methods, the restaurants operate under the tagline Saving the Earth, One Pizza at a Time.<ref name="tag">{{cite web| title = A pizza joint Al Gore could love might be coming here| work = Columbus Dispatch |first= Bill |last= Chronister | url = http://www.dispatch.com/live/content/business/stories/2008/01/22/rest_0122.ART_ART_01-22-08_C10_7494FC7.html |date= 22 January 2008}}</ref>

==Overview==
Pizza Fusion was founded in 2006 by Michael Gordon and Vaughan Lazar. Lazar and Gordon had targeted [[Newman's Own]], [[Patagonia (clothing)|Patagonia]], and [[Starbucks]] as role models, before opening their first store.<ref name="entrepreneur">{{cite web| title = Pizza Fusion| publisher =Entrepreneur.com |first= Sara |last= Wilson |date= May 2008 | url = http://www.entrepreneur.com/magazine/entrepreneur/2008/may/192566.html}}</ref>  In April 2009, the company signed a franchise agreement for [[Saudi Arabia]] with the Samir Group, a Saudi Arabia-based conglomerate.<ref name="Samir">{{cite web| title = US Pizza Fusion reaches Saudi Arabian franchise deal| publisher = Insidefranchising.net| date = 3 April 2009| url = http://www.insidefranchising.net/IFNewsletter/fullstoryIF.asp?news_id=1235&cat_id=2| url-status = dead| archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20110723154915/http://www.insidefranchising.net/IFNewsletter/fullstoryIF.asp?news_id=1235&cat_id=2| archivedate = 23 July 2011}}</ref>

It specializes in oval-shaped [[gourmet]] pizzas,<ref name="oval">{{cite web| title = Fort Lauderdale-based Pizza Fusion committed to saving the world| work= South Florida Sentinel| first= Arlene |last= Satchell |date=1 May 2008| url = http://www.chicagotribune.com/features/green/sfl-flzpizzafusion0502sbmay02,0,7307034.story}}</ref> salads, sandwiches, and beverages made from natural ingredients &mdash; along with [[gluten-free]], [[vegan]], and [[lactose]]-free menu items.<ref name="oval"/><ref name="success"/>

At its peak, in 2008, the restaurant chain had 63 locations<ref name="success">{{cite web| title = Pizza Fusion's First Year of National Franchising a Success| publisher = Pizza Fusion |date= February 11, 2008 |url = http://www.prweb.com/releases/2008/02/prweb688304.htm}}</ref> including those in California, Colorado, Connecticut, Florida, New Jersey, Ohio, as well as in [[Jeddah]] and [[Riyadh]], [[Saudi Arabia]].<ref name="locations">{{cite web| title = Pizza Fusion's Locations| publisher = Pizzafusion.com |accessdate=9 January 2011 |url = http://www.pizzafusion.com/locations/}}</ref> Although it had projected growing to 300 stores by 2010,<ref name="entrepreneur"/> as of 2011 the chain was down to 13 locations.<ref name="locations2">{{cite web| title = Pizza Fusion Locations| publisher = Pizzafusion.com| accessdate = 19 August 2011| url = http://franchise.pizzafusion.com/franchise-opportunity/current-locations.aspx| url-status = dead| archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20110823104609/http://www.franchise.pizzafusion.com/franchise-opportunity/current-locations.aspx| archivedate = 23 August 2011}}</ref>

==Green building methods==
{{More citations needed|section|date=May 2011}}
Green construction materials and techniques used by the restaurant include a website powered by renewable energy, 100 percent post-consumer paper products, countertops remanufactured from plastic detergent bottles, wall insulation remanufactured from recycled blue jeans, 100% post-consumer drywall, non-VOC paint ([[volatile organic compounds]]), floor finishes with low VOC concrete sealant or renewable resources such as bamboo or cork, tiles from recycled glass bottles, low-flow toilets and sinks, Forest Steward Council wood, energy efficientâ€”compact fluorescent lighting, super-efficient air conditioning and a heat exchange system that recaptures heat from the pizza ovens for the domestic hot water needs.<ref name="breaking"/>

The chain offsets 100% of the power consumption in its locations by using purchasing [[carbon credits]] and using environmentally sustainable building practices.  The restaurant delivery drivers use [[hybrid electric vehicles]].
