'''Levi Horace Bancroft''' (December 26, 1861 â€“ September 5, 1948) was a politician and jurist in the State of [[Wisconsin]].

==Biography==
Levi H. Bancroft was born on December 26, 1861 to George I. and Helen M. Bancroft; reports have differed on the location. He attended high school in [[Lone Rock, Wisconsin]] and later became a teacher. In 1884, he graduated from the [[University of Wisconsin Law School]].<ref>{{cite book |chapter-url=https://books.google.com/books?id=AEwNAAAAIAAJ&pg=PA1129 |chapter=Biographical Sketches |title=Blue Book of the State of Wisconsin |year=1909 |publisher=[[Wisconsin|State of Wisconsin]] |page=1129 |accessdate=2020-06-24 |via=Google Books}}</ref> On June 11, 1890, Bancroft married Myrtle DeLap. From 1907 to 1913, he was a [[judge advocate]] of what is now the [[Wisconsin Army National Guard]].

He died at his farm near [[Richland Center, Wisconsin|Richland Center]] on September 5, 1948.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ci.richland-center.wi.us/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Bancroft-House-2.pdf|title=Levi H. and Myrtle Bancroft House, 277 N. Sheldon Street|publisher=City of Richland Center|accessdate=2016-04-29}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/54080494/judge-levi-h-bancroft-dies-at-age-of-87/ |title=Judge Levi H. Bancroft Dies At Age of 87 |newspaper=[[The Capital Times]] |location=Richland Center, Wisconsin |page=1 |date=1948-09-07 |access-date=2020-06-24 |via=Newspapers.com}}</ref>

==Political career==
Bancroft served as the District Attorney of [[Richland County, Wisconsin]] from 1886 to 1888, [[Richland Center, Wisconsin]] City Attorney for six years, and City Supervisor. From 1897 to 1902, he was a county judge of Richland County. Bancroft was a delegate to the [[1900 Republican National Convention]]. In addition, he was delegate to a number of State Republican Conventions. He would serve as an Assistant Attorney General for a disputed amount of time. Bancroft was a member of the Wisconsin State Assembly from 1907 to 1910, serving as Speaker in 1909 and 1910. From 1911 to 1913, he was Attorney General of Wisconsin, and he was a [[Wisconsin circuit courts|Wisconsin Circuit Court]] judge. Sources are varied over his length of service. Later, he served as a U.S. Attorney in Wisconsin from 1927 to 1932. From 1934 to 1936, Bancroft was Mayor of Richland Center.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://digicoll.library.wisc.edu/cgi/f/findaid/findaid-idx?c=wiarchives;view=reslist;subview=standard;didno=uw-whs-pltv000y;focusrgn=bioghist;cc=wiarchives;byte=491276492|title=Levi H. Bancroft Papers, 1863-1956|publisher=University of Wisconsin Digital Collections|accessdate=2016-04-30}}</ref> He was again a county judge of Richland County from 1938 to 1948.
