'''Charles Samuel Everett''' (17 June 1901 – 10 October 1970)<ref name="CAprofile">{{cite web|url=http://www.cricketarchive.co.uk/Archive/Players/5/5843/5843.html|title=Sam Everett|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=5 February 2009}}</ref> was an Australian [[cricket]]er who played [[first-class cricket]] for [[New South Wales cricket team|New South Wales]] from 1921 to 1930.<ref name="CAprofile" />

Everett was born in Sydney and educated at [[Newington College]] (1917–1918).<ref>Newington College Register of Past Students 1863–1998 (Syd, 1999) pp 61</ref> A right-arm [[fast bowling|fast medium bowler]] and left-handed batsman for [[Petersham, New South Wales|Petersham]] Cricket Club,<ref name="Pollard">Pollard (1988), p.434.</ref> Everett took 134 wickets at first-class level at an [[Bowling average|average]] of 27.11 runs per wicket.<ref name="CAprofile" /> He was chosen as part of the Australian squad to [[Australian cricket team in England in 1926|tour in England in 1926]] but illness and poor form meant he missed selection for the [[Test cricket|Test matches]].<ref name="Pollard"/>

His best [[bowling analysis|bowling figures]], 6/23, were taken against [[Queensland Bulls|Queensland]] in January 1930; in the same match [[Donald Bradman]] scored his then-record 452.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cricketarchive.co.uk/Archive/Scorecards/13/13341.html|title=New South Wales v Queensland : Sheffield Shield 1929/30|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=5 February 2009}}</ref>
