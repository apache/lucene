'''Jonathan Freeman''' (March 21, 1745 – August 20, 1808) was an [[United States|American]] politician and a [[United States Representative]] from [[New Hampshire]].

==Early life==
Born in [[Mansfield, Connecticut]], Freeman attended the public schools and moved to New Hampshire in 1769, settling in [[Hanover, New Hampshire|Hanover]].

==Career==
Freeman engaged in agricultural pursuits and was town clerk from March 1778 to December 1787.<ref>{{cite book|title=The Records of the Town of Hanover, New Hampshire 1761-1818 : the Records of Town Meetings and of the Selectmen, Comprising All of the First Volume of Records and Being Volume 1 of the Printed Records of the Town, Volume 1|year=1905|publisher=The Town, 1905 - Hanover (N.H. : Town)|url=https://archive.org/details/recordstownhano00nhgoog |page=[https://archive.org/details/recordstownhano00nhgoog/page/n340 329]|accessdate=25 July 2014}}</ref> He was also [[justice of the peace]] and, from 1789 to 1797, [[Executive Council of New Hampshire|executive councilor]]. He was Treasurer of Dartmouth College from 1789 to 1808 as well as a Trustee of the College from 1793 to 1808.<ref>{{cite web|title=Jonathan Freeman|url=http://www.rootsweb.ancestry.com/~nyrensse/bio58.htm|publisher=Ancestry.com|accessdate=2 January 2014}}</ref>

A member of the [[New Hampshire House of Representatives]] from 1787 to 1789, Freeman also served in the [[New Hampshire State Senate|State senate]] from 1789 to 1794. He was a delegate to the Constitutional convention of 1791, and a member of the State council.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Huntington Family Association, Huntington, Huntington|first1=Samuel, Richard Thomas|title=The Huntington Family in America: A Genealogical Memoir of the Known Descendants of Simon Huntington from 1633 to 1915, Including Those who Have Retained the Family Name, and Many Bearing Other Surnames|year=1915|publisher=Huntington family association, 1915|page=[https://archive.org/details/huntingtonfamily1915hunt/page/289 289]|url=https://archive.org/details/huntingtonfamily1915hunt|accessdate=25 July 2014}}</ref>

Elected as a [[Federalist Party (United States)|Federalist]] to the [[5th United States Congress|Fifth]] and [[6th United States Congress|Sixth]] Congresses, Freeman served as [[United States House of Representatives|United States Representative]] for the state of New Hampshire from March 4, 1797 to March 3, 1801.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Capace|first1=Nancy|title=Encyclopedia of New Hampshire|publisher=North American Book Dist LLC, Jan 1, 2001 - New Hampshire|page=393|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=FyCVd_nCYl8C&pg=PA393&dq=Jonathan+Freeman+(representative)&hl=en&sa=X&ei=x1DSU9PFBIKYyASkqIHoAg&ved=0CCoQ6AEwAg#v=onepage&q=Jonathan%20Freeman%20(representative)&f=false|accessdate=25 July 2014}}</ref> After his service, he resumed agricultural pursuits.

==Death==
Freeman died in Hanover on August 20, 1808 (age 63 years, 152 days). He is [[burial|interred]] at Hanover Center Cemetery, Hanover, New Hampshire.

==Family life==
Son of Edmund and Martha Otis Freeman,<ref>{{cite web|title=Jonathan Freeman|url=http://wc.rootsweb.ancestry.com/cgi-bin/igm.cgi?op=GET&db=oldmankew&id=I5201|publisher=2012 Ancestry.com|accessdate=2 January 2014}}</ref> Freeman married Sarah Huntington on February 2, 177,<ref>{{cite web|title=Jonathan Freeman|url=http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/~dav4is/ODTs/FREEMAN.shtml#FRMN133|publisher=Freeman Families|accessdate=2 January 2014}}</ref> and she bore eleven children of whom nine lived beyond infancy, Peyton R., Jonathan, Christopher, Edward, Sarah, Asa, Samuel, and Hanna.<ref>{{cite book|title=Freeman Genealogy|year=1875|publisher=Boston: Franklin Press: Rand, Avery, and Company 1875|page=[https://archive.org/details/freemangenealogy1875free/page/126 126]|url=https://archive.org/details/freemangenealogy1875free|accessdate=25 July 2014}}</ref> His nephew, [[Nathaniel Freeman, Jr.]], was also a U.S. Representative from Massachusetts.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Scales|first1=John|title=History of Strafford County, New Hampshire and Representative Citizens|year=1914|publisher=Richmond-Arnold, 1914 - Strafford County (N.H.)|page=[https://archive.org/details/historyofstraffo00scal_0/page/38 38]|url=https://archive.org/details/historyofstraffo00scal_0|accessdate=25 July 2014}}</ref>
