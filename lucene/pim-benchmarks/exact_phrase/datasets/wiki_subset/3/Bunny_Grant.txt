'''George Leslie "Bunny" Grant''' (29 September 1940 – 1 November 2018) was a [[Jamaican people|Jamaican]] [[professional]] [[featherweight|feather]]/[[super featherweight|super feather]]/[[lightweight|light]]/[[light welterweight|light welter]]/[[welterweight]]/[[light middleweight]] [[Boxing|boxer]] of the 1950s, '60s and '70s who won the Jamaican lightweight title, Jamaican welterweight title, Central American light welterweight Title, Latin American junior welterweight title, and [[List of Commonwealth Boxing Council Champions#Lightweight|British Commonwealth lightweight title]], and was a challenger for the [[World Boxing Council]] (WBC) light welterweight title and [[World Boxing Association]] (WBA) World light welterweight title against [[Eddie Perkins]], Commonwealth welterweight title against [[Clyde Gray]], his professional fighting weight varied from {{convert|125+1/2|lb|kg}}, i.e. [[featherweight]] to {{convert|152+1/2|lb|kg}}, i.e. [[light middleweight]].<ref name="Statistics at boxrec.com">{{cite web |url=http://boxrec.com/list_bouts.php?human_id=15935&cat=boxer |title=Bunny Grant statistics at boxrec.com |publisher=boxrec.com |date=31 December 2012 |accessdate=1 January 2013}}</ref> Bunny Grant was managed by Jacques Deschamps, and Pancho Rankine (circa 1962), and trained by Harry Wiley<ref>{{cite web|url=http://boxrec.com/media/index.php/Harry_Wiley|title=Harry Wiley - BoxRec|website=boxrec.com}}</ref> (circa 1962).<ref name="Biography at boxrec.com">{{cite web |url=http://boxrec.com/media/index.php?title=Human:15935 |title=Bunny Grant biography at boxrec.com |publisher=boxrec.com |date=31 December 2012 |accessdate=1 January 2013}}</ref> He died on 1 November 2018.
