The '''2013–14 All-Ireland Senior Club Hurling Championship''' was the 44th staging of the All-Ireland championship since its establishment in 1970. The championship began on 13 October 2013 and ended on 17 March 2014.

[[St. Thomas's GAA|St. Thomas's]] were the [[2012–13 All-Ireland Senior Club Hurling Championship|defending champions]], however, they failed to emerge from the county championship in Galway.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.irishexaminer.com/archives/2013/0318/ireland/might-and-fight-seal-historic-day-for-st-thomasapos-225720.html|title=Might and fight seal historic day for St Thomas’|date=18 March 2013|publisher=Irish Examiner|accessdate=2 October 2013|first=Diarmuid|last=O'Flynn}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.irishexaminer.com/archives/2013/1014/ireland/canning-sends-st-thomasapos-tumbling-246219.html|title=Canning sends St Thomas’ tumbling|date=14 October 2013|publisher=Irish Examiner|accessdate=14 October 2013|first=Declan|last=Rooney}}</ref>

===Miscellaneous===

* [[Mount Leinster Rangers GAA|Mount Leinster Rangers]] win the Leinster title for the first time in their history. It is the first time that any team from Carlow has claimed a Leinster title at senior level.
* [[Oulart the Ballagh GAA|Oulart-the Ballagh]] lose the Leinster final for the fourth consecutive year.
