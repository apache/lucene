'''Applicat-Prazan''' gallery was established in 1993 on the [[Left Bank]] in the [[Saint-Germain-des-Prés]] area of [[Paris]] at 16 rue de Seine (75006 Paris, France) under the initiative of Bernard Prazan, a keen art collector.
[[File:Logo Applicat-Prazan 2017.png|thumb|Logo Applicat-Prazan]]
[[Franck Prazan]], Bernard Prazan's son and former managing director of [[Christie's]] in France, has been running the gallery since 2004.

In 2010 a second location was added on the [[Right Bank]], at 14 avenue Matignon, 75008 Paris.

In 2013, the gallery celebrated its 20th anniversary of its inception with a monographic exhibition dedicated to [[Serge Poliakoff]].<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Robert|first1=Martine|title=La galerie Applicat-Prazan célbre son vingtièrme anniversaire|journal=Les Echos|date=13 May 2013|url=http://www.lesechos.fr/13/05/2013/LesEchos/21434-091-ECH_la-galerie-applicat-prazan-celebre-son-vingtieme-anniversaire.htm}}</ref>

Since its foundation the gallery specializes exclusively in the important [[School of Paris|post-war European painters]] and specifically in the most significant artists of this period such as   : [[Jean Dubuffet]], [[Maurice Estève]], [[Jean Fautrier]], [[Hans Hartung]], [[Jean Hélion]], [[Wifredo Lam]], [[Alberto Magnelli]], [[Alfred Manessier]], [[André Masson]], [[Georges Mathieu]], [[Serge Poliakoff]], [[Jean-Paul Riopelle]], [[Pierre Soulages]], [[Nicolas de Staël]], [[Maria Elena Vieira da Silva]] and [[Zao Wou-Ki]].
