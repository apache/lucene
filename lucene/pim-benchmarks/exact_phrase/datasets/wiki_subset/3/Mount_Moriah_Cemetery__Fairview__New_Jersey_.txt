'''Mount Moriah Cemetery''' is a burial ground in [[Fairview, Bergen County, New Jersey]] in the United States, located on the western slopes of the [[Hudson Palisades]], nearby the [[Fairview Cemetery (Fairview, New Jersey)|Fairview Cemetery]].<ref>{{cite web   | title = Mt. Moriah Jewish Cemetery   | work = Bergen County: Fairview   | publisher = Jewish Cemetery Project.   | date = 2009   | url = http://www.iajgsjewishcemeteryproject.org/new-jersey/fairview-bergen-county.html   | accessdate = 2013-10-02}}</ref>

[[Moriah]] ({{Hebrew Name|hebrew={{Script/Hebrew|מוֹרִיָּה}} |stan=Moriyya |tiber=Môriyyā}}) ([[Arabic language|Arabic]]: مروة ''Marwah'') is the name given to a mountain by the [[Book of Genesis]], in which context it is the location of the [[Binding of Isaac|sacrifice of Isaac]].

==Notable interments==
*Pesach Ackerman (1928–2013), Rabbi of [[Meseritz Synagogue]]<ref>[http://thevillager.com/2013/06/20/rabbi-pesach-ackerman-of-anshe-meseritz-dies-at-84/ East Villager]</ref>
*[[Camilla Frydan]] (1887–1949), Austrian-born pianist, singer, composer and song writer
*Robert P. Grover (1916–1942), first Jewish serviceman from Jersey City to die in WWll<ref>[http://www.njcu.edu/programs/jchistory/Pages/G_Pages/Grover_Park.html NJCU:Robert P. Grover]</ref>
*Russell Harding (d. 2008), New York City administrator<ref>[https://www.nytimes.com/2012/10/08/nyregion/russell-hardings-blog-chronicled-his-fall-from-grace-and-plans-for-suicide.html?_r=0 New York Times October 7, 2012]</ref>
*[[Arthur George Klein]] (1904–1968), United States Representative (NY)<ref>[http://www.findagrave.com/cgi-bin/fg.cgi?page=cr&CRid=100116 Find a Grave]</ref>
*[[Ira Rubin]] (1930–2013), American professional [[contract bridge]] player<ref name=NJ>{{cite web |title=Ira Rubin, world champion bridge player, dies at 82 |url=http://www.northjersey.com/obituaries/190152811_Ira_Rubin__world_champion_bridge_player__dies_at_82.html |publisher=([[Bergen Record]]) North Jersey.com |author=Levin, Jay |date=February 7, 2013 |accessdate=2013-02-09}}</ref>
