'''''Tishreen''''' ({{lang-ar|تشرين}}) is one of the state-owned [[Arabic]] daily newspapers published in [[Syria]].<ref>{{cite journal|author1=Judith Pies|author2=Philip Madanat|title=Media Accountability Practices Online in Syria|journal=MediaAct|date=June 2011|issue=10/2011|url=http://www.mediaact.eu/fileadmin/user_upload/WP4/WP4_Syria.pdf}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|author1=David Commins|author2=David W. Lesch|title=Historical Dictionary of Syria|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=wpBWAgAAQBAJ&pg=PA252|date=5 December 2013|publisher=Scarecrow Press|isbn=978-0-8108-7966-9|page=253}}</ref> The daily is based in [[Damascus]].<ref name=syr>{{cite book|title=Mass Media in the Middle East: A Comprehensive Handbook|year=1994|publisher=Greenwood Press|location=Westport, CT|page=265|url=https://www.questia.com/read/23321662/mass-media-in-the-middle-east-a-comprehensive-handbook|author=Arvind Singhal|author2=Vijay Krishna|editor=Yahya R. Kamalipour|editor2=Hamid Mowlana|chapter=Syria}}{{Subscription required|via=Questia}}</ref> 

==History and profile==
''Tishreen'' was first published in October 1975<ref>{{cite web|author=Salam Kawakibi|title=The Private Media in Syria|url=http://acimedit.be/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/The-private-media-in-Syria.pdf|publisher=University of Amsterdam and Hivos|accessdate=16 September 2014|year=2010}}</ref> in addition to two other state-owned dailies, namely ''[[Al-Ba'ath|Al Baath]]'' and ''[[Al-Thawra (newspaper)|Al Thawra]]'', which were launched earlier.<ref name=George2003>{{cite book|author=Alan George|title=Syria: Neither Bread Nor Freedom|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=dFdbVVcKsSIC&pg=PA125|date=6 September 2003|publisher=Zed Books|isbn=978-1-84277-213-3|page=125}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Media sustainability index 2006/2007|url=http://www.irex.org/system/files/MENA-MSI06-Syria.pdf|work=IREX|accessdate=2 October 2013}}</ref> The daily was named after [[Yom Kippur War|Arab-Israeli war]] in October 1973.<ref name=George2003/><ref>{{cite book|author=Miriam Cooke|title=Dissident Syria: Making Oppositional Arts Official|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=VsaC40CnhcoC&pg=PA8|date=14 August 2007|publisher=Duke University Press|isbn=0-8223-4035-6|page=8}}</ref> Tishreen Organization for Press and Publishing is the former publisher of the daily.<ref name=George2003/> Tishreen Organization also published ''[[Syria Times]]'', a defunct English daily and a current e-newspaper.<ref name=George2003/> Later Al Wahda institution became the publisher of both publications in addition to ''Al Thawra''.<ref>{{cite news|title=Syria's Pavilion Best at Tehran's International Exhibition of the Press and News Agencies|url=http://www.thefreelibrary.com/Syria's+Pavilion+Best+at+Tehran's+International+Exhibition+of+the...-a0271489275|accessdate=3 October 2013|newspaper=Syrian Arab News Agency|date=3 November 2011}}</ref> 

In 1997, ''Tishreen'' launched its website.<ref>{{cite journal|author=Alan George|title=Syrian Surfers Take to the Net|journal=The Middle East|date=October 2000|url=https://www.questia.com/read/1G1-66211063/syrian-surfers-take-to-the-net}}{{Subscription required|via=Questia}}</ref> The 1992 circulation of the paper was 75,000 copies.<ref name=syr/> Daily circulation of ''Tishreen'' was nearly 60,000 in the mid-2000s.<ref name=George2003/> The paper's online version  was the 48ht most visited website for 2010 in the [[MENA region]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Forbes Releases Top 50 MENA Online Newspapers; Lebanon Fails to Make Top 10|url=http://www.jadaoun.com/2803/forbes-releases-top-50-mena-online-newspapers-lebanon-fails-to-make-top-10/|accessdate=11 September 2014|work=Jad Aoun|date=28 October 2010}}</ref>

Although the daily is owned by the state, it has a critical stance on local news, especially in regard to [[corruption]] and mismanagement.<ref>{{cite web|title=Syria|url=http://www.arabpressnetwork.org/newspaysv2.php?id=138|work=The Arab Press Network|accessdate=2 October 2013|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120825124135/http://www.arabpressnetwork.org/newspaysv2.php?id=138|archivedate=25 August 2012|df=dmy-all}}</ref> By 2004 the paper became a platform to support for the charities in the country along with ''Al Thawra''.<ref>{{cite book|title=Civil Society and the State in Syria: The Outsourcing of Social Responsibility|year=2012|url=http://hal.archives-ouvertes.fr/docs/00/69/86/80/PDF/FINAL_Saint_Andrews_Papers.pdf|author=Laura Ruiz de Elvira|chapter=State-Charities Relations in Syria: between Reinforcement, Control and Coercion}}</ref> 

Mohammad Kheir Al Wadi served as the editor-in-chief of ''Tishreen''.<ref name=rw>{{cite web|title=Muslim Anti-Semitism: A Clear and Present Danger|url=http://users.ipfw.edu/bartky/Y200Y401%20Judaism/Judaism%20Course-Wistrich%20on%20Muslim%20Anti-Semitism.pdf|work=IPFW|accessdate=6 October 2013|author=Robert S. Wistrich}}</ref> As of 2005 the editor-in-chief was Khalaf Al Jarrad.<ref>{{cite journal|author=Shmuel Bar|title=Bashar's Syria: The Regime and its Strategic Worldview|journal=Comparative Strategy|date=2006 |volume=25|issue=5 |url=https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/10.1080/01495930601105412}}</ref> Samira Al Masalmeh was appointed to the post in January 2012.<ref name=nem>{{cite web|title=The Norwegian Embassy meets Tishreen newspaper's female editor-in-chief|url=http://www.norway-sy.org/News_and_events/culture/The-Norwegian-Embassy-meets-Tishreen-newspapers-female-editor-in-chief/#.UkwybmRJVgs|work=Norwegian Embassy|accessdate=2 October 2013|date=16 February 2012}}</ref> She is the first female editor-in-chief in the country.<ref name=nem/> ''Tishreen'' also has an English news portal, ''Syria Millennium'', which is accessed through its website.<ref>{{cite news|title=Syria Millennium|url=http://tishreen.news.sy/tishreen/public/syria-millennium|accessdate=2 October 2013|newspaper=Tishreen}}</ref>

==Contents==
Mohammad Kheir Al Wadi, then editor-in-chief, wrote in January 2000 "Zionism created the Holocaust myth to blackmail and terrorize the world's intellectuals and politicians."<ref name=rw/>

==Incidents==
The website of ''Tishreen'' was hacked by unknown groups in late April 2011.<ref>{{cite news|title=Websites of Tishreen Newspaper and People's Assembly Hacked|url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-255136316.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131011163017/http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-255136316.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=11 October 2013|accessdate=3 October 2013|work=SANA|date=29 April 2011}}{{Subscription required|via=Highbeam}}</ref> In December 2012, Naji Assaad, a journalist for the daily, was assassinated in Damascus allegedly by opposition forces who have been [[Syrian civil war|fighting]] against the Assad government in the country since 2011.<ref>{{cite news|title=Pro-government newspaper journalist killed in Syria|url=http://www.dc4mf.org/en/content/pro-government-newspaper-journalist-killed-syria|accessdate=2 October 2013|work=Doha Centre for Media Freedom|date=5 December 2012}}</ref>
