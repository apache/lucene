'''Percival "Percy" Hayles''' (24 October 1940 &ndash; 28 August 1978 (aged 37)) was a [[Jamaican people|Jamaican]] [[professional]] [[lightweight|light]]/[[light welterweight|light welter]]/[[welterweight]] [[Boxing|boxer]] of the 1950s, '60s and '70s who won the [[List of Commonwealth Boxing Council Champions#lightweight|British Commonwealth lightweight title]], and was a challenger for the Jamaican lightweight title against [[Bunny Grant]], and [[World Boxing Council]] (WBC) light welterweight title and [[World Boxing Association]] (WBA) World light welterweight title against [[Carlos Morocho Hern√°ndez|Carlos "Morocho" Enrique Hernandez Ramos]], his professional fighting weight varied from {{convert|132|lb|kg stlb||abbr=on}}, i.e. [[lightweight]] to {{convert|142|lb|kg stlb||abbr=on}}, i.e. [[welterweight]].<ref name="Statistics at boxrec.com">{{cite web|url=http://boxrec.com/list_bouts.php?human_id=18719&cat=boxer|title=Statistics at boxrec.com|publisher=boxrec.com|date=31 December 2012|accessdate=1 January 2013}}</ref> He was the 1964 Men's Winner of the [[Jamaica Sportsperson of the year]], he was killed when he was hit by a car while riding a bicycle near [[Kingston, Jamaica]].<ref name="Biography at boxrec.com">{{cite web|url=http://boxrec.com/media/index.php?title=Human:18719|title=Biography at boxrec.com|publisher=boxrec.com|date=31 December 2012|accessdate=1 January 2013}}</ref>
