'''C. N. Ahmad Moulavi''' (1905 – 1993) was an Indian writer of [[Malayalam literature]], best known as the translator of the first complete publication of [[Quran]] in [[Malayalam]].<ref name="Osella">{{cite journal |last1=Filippo Osella & Caroline Osella |title=Islamism and Social Reform in Kerala, South India |journal=Modern Asian Studies |year=2008 |volume=42 |issue=2/3 |page=327 |jstor=20488022}}</ref> He was the author of a number of books on [[Islam]] and was reported to have contributed to the propagation of education among the Muslims of [[Malabar region]]. A member of the [[Kerala Sahitya Akademi]] during the period 1959–64, Moulavi was honoured by the academy with the [[Kerala Sahitya Akademi Fellowship|distinguished fellowship]] in 1989.

== Biography ==
[[File:Tomb of A'la Hadrat Shah Abdul Wahhab.jpg|right|thumb|Baqiyat Salihat Arabic College]]
C. N. Ahmad Moulavi was born in 1905 at Cheroor, a village in [[Eranad]] in [[Malappuram district]] of the south Indian state of [[Kerala]] to Nathankodan Assan Kutty and Azhuvath Khatheeja.<ref name="Biography on Kerala Sahitya Akademi portal">{{Cite web |url=http://www.keralasahityaakademi.org/sp/Writers/Profiles/CNAhmedMaulavi/Html/CNAMaulavigraphy.htm |title=Biography on Kerala Sahitya Akademi portal |date=2019-04-05 |website=Kerala Sahitya Akademi |access-date=2019-04-05 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190406113421/http://www.keralasahityaakademi.org/sp/Writers/Profiles/CNAhmedMaulavi/Html/CNAMaulavigraphy.htm |archive-date=6 April 2019 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Born in a family of poor financial means, he lost his father early and could have primary education for only three years after which he had to work in a farm for sustenance.<ref name="Miller2015">{{cite book|author=Roland E. Miller|title=Mappila Muslim Culture: How a Historic Muslim Community in India Has Blended Tradition and Modernity|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=XjSzCAAAQBAJ&pg=PA236|date=27 April 2015|publisher=SUNY Press|isbn=978-1-4384-5602-7|pages=236–}}</ref> At the age of sixteen, he went back to studies, first at [[Karuvarakundu]] Grand Dars of Kunjalam Musaliar and later under Kattukandan Kunjahmed Musaliar.<ref name="Datta1987">{{cite book|author=Amaresh Datta|title=Encyclopaedia of Indian Literature: A-Devo|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ObFCT5_taSgC&pg=PA106|year=1987|publisher=Sahitya Akademi|isbn=978-81-260-1803-1|pages=106–}}</ref> This was followed by a short stints at the Madras Jamalia Arabic College and Bombay where he studied under K. T. Ibrahim Moulavi, only to proceed to [[Baqiyat Salihat Arabic College]], Vellore in 1928 from where he passed ''Afsal Ul Ulama'' examination in 1931. On his return to Kerala, he joined the Malappuram Training School as a religious instructor but resigned from the job in 1944 to start his own clothing business.<ref name="Miller2015" />

Moulavi started a magazine by name, ''Ansari'' which became defunct after publishing 14 issues; though he restarted the magazine under a new name, ''New Ansari'' in 1955, the publication did not last long.<ref name="Biography on Kerala Sahitya Akademi portal" /> When the ''East Eranad Education Society'' was formed in 1964, Moulavi became the founder president of the society; the society, which started [[Dr. Gafoor Memorial MES Mampad College]] in 1965, was handed over the [[Muslim Educational Society]] (MES) in 1967.<ref name="MES Mampad College">{{Cite web |url=http://www.mesmampad.org |title=MES Mampad College |date=2019-04-06 |website=mesmampad.org |access-date=2019-04-06}}</ref>

Maulavi died on 27 April 1993, at the age of 88, at [[Kozhikode]].<ref name="Biography on Kerala Sahitya Akademi portal" />

== Legacy and honours ==
Maulavi published twelve books on Islamic studies, including a reportedly controversial book, ''Islamile Dhanavitharanapadhathi'' (Distribution of Wealth in Islam Tradition).<ref name="List of works">{{Cite web |url=http://www.keralasahityaakademi.org/sp/Writers/Profiles/CNAhmedMaulavi/Html/CNAbdulmaulavibooks.htm |title=List of works |date=2019-04-05 |website=Kerala Sahitya Akademi |access-date=2019-04-05 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190406074525/http://www.keralasahityaakademi.org/sp/Writers/Profiles/CNAhmedMaulavi/Html/CNAbdulmaulavibooks.htm |archive-date=6 April 2019 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He was the first to publish a complete translation of the [[Quran]] into Malayalam<ref name="Kupferschmidt1987">{{cite book|author=Uri M. Kupferschmidt|title=The Supreme Muslim Council: Islam Under the British Mandate for Palestine|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ChEVAAAAIAAJ&pg=PA462|year=1987|publisher=BRILL|isbn=90-04-07929-7|pages=462–}}</ref> which he published in 1963,<ref name="C N Ahmed Maulavi: The Malayali Pioneer in Quran Translation">{{Cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4pcGd0FKHc0 |title=C N Ahmed Maulavi: The Malayali Pioneer in Quran Translation |last=MediaoneTV Live |date=2014-07-03 |access-date=2019-04-06}}</ref> at a time when translation of Quran was considered a blasphemy.<ref name="Miller2015" /> He wrote on many contentious issues such as divorce and maintenance among Muslims<ref name="Engineer2008">{{cite book|author=Asgharali Engineer|title=The Rights of Women in Islam|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Q3uB_umOEbwC&pg=PA169|year=2008|publisher=Sterling Publishers Pvt. Ltd|isbn=978-81-207-3933-8|pages=169–}}</ref> and his book, ''Mahathaya Mappila Sahitya Paramparyam'' (The glorious Mappila Literary Heritage)<ref name="Mahathaya Mappila Sahitya Paramparyam">{{Cite web |url=https://kithabi.com/books/mahathaya-mappila-sahitya-paramparyam-%E0%B4%AE%E0%B4%B9%E0%B4%A4%E0%B5%8D%E0%B4%A4%E0%B4%BE%E0%B4%AF-%E0%B4%AE%E0%B4%BE%E0%B4%AA%E0%B5%8D%E0%B4%AA%E0%B4%BF%E0%B4%B3-%E0%B4%B8%E0%B4%BE%E0%B4%B9/ |title=Mahathaya Mappila Sahitya Paramparyam |date=2019-04-06 |website=kithabi.com |access-date=2019-04-06}}</ref> is a comprehensive work on the Muslim literature in Kerala<ref name="Datta1987" /><ref name="Mohammed2007">{{cite book|author=U. Mohammed|title=Educational Empowerment of Kerala Muslims: A Socio-historical Perspective|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=PCBdogPnnqsC&pg=PA30|year=2007|publisher=Other Books|isbn=978-81-903887-3-3|pages=30–}}</ref> with details about the contribution of Muslims in the development of Arabic-Malayalam literature.<ref name="Contribution of Muslims in the Development of Malayalam Language and Literature">{{Cite web |url=https://www.ummid.com/news/2011/March/16.03.2011/contribution_of_muslims_fr_malayalam_lang.htm |title=Contribution of Muslims in the Development of Malayalam Language and Literature |website=www.ummid.com |access-date=2019-04-06}}</ref> He served as a member of the [[Kerala Sahitya Akademi]] for five years from 1959 to 1964. In 1989, he was inducted as a [[Kerala Sahitya Akademi Fellowship|distinguished fellow]] by the [[Kerala Sahitya Akademi|academy]].<ref name="Kerala Sahitya Akademi Fellowship">{{Cite web |url=http://www.keralasahityaakademi.org/sp/Writers/Fellows/Html/HonoraryList.htm |title=Kerala Sahitya Akademi Fellowship |date=2019-04-06 |website=Kerala Sahitya Akademi |access-date=2019-04-06 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190331092627/http://www.keralasahityaakademi.org/sp/Writers/Fellows/Html/HonoraryList.htm |archive-date=31 March 2019 |url-status=dead }}</ref> His birth centenary was celebrated in Kozhikode in 2005 and an exhibition of all his writings were organized as a part of it.<ref name="The Hindu : Kerala News : In remembrance">{{Cite web |url=https://www.thehindu.com/2005/12/26/stories/2005122610910300.htm |title=The Hindu : Kerala News : In remembrance |website=www.thehindu.com |access-date=2019-04-06}}</ref>
