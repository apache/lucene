'''Antti Ilari Törmänen''' (born September 19, 1970) is a retired professional [[ice hockey]] player and current head coach of [[EHC Biel]] in Switzerland's [[National League (ice hockey)|National League]]. He played for the [[Ottawa Senators]], [[Jokerit]], [[HV71]] and [[Södertälje SK]] over 14 seasons.

==Playing career==
Törmänen had been playing professionally for [[Jokerit]] for four seasons when he was drafted in the [[1994 NHL Entry Draft]] in the eleventh round, 274th overall by the [[Ottawa Senators]] of the NHL. After one more season with Jokerit, he signed with the Senators for the [[1995–96 Ottawa Senators season|1995–96 season]]. Törmänen played in 50 games for the Senators, and 22 games with the [[Prince Edward Island Senators]]. This was his only season in North America. After the season, he returned to Europe where he returned to Jokerit, and also played for HV71 Jönköping and Södertälje SK until retiring after the 2003–04 season. His best season statistically was 1993–94 with Jokerit where he scored 20 goals and 18 assists in 46 games.

As a player, he was nicknamed "Törminator".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sodertaljesk.se/2002_team.asp?PID=48 |title=Antti Törmänen |publisher=Södertälje SK |language=Swedish |date=2003 |accessdate=5 December 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151208113002/http://www.sodertaljesk.se/2002_team.asp?PID=48 |archivedate=8 December 2015 }}</ref>

==Coaching career==
Törmänen coached [[Espoo Blues]] U18 and U20 teams, winning U20 title in 2009. He then moved to [[Jokerit]] and was assistant coach of the team, before being sacked mid-season along with head coach [[Hannu Aravirta]]. In the 2010–11 season he was the head coach of [[Vaasan Sport|Sport]] in Finland's second-tier [[Mestis]], guiding the team to the title, while being named Mestis Coach of the Year.

He joined [[SC Bern]] of the Swiss [[National League A]] (NLA) as an assistant coach for the 2010–11 season and on October 21, 2011, he was promoted from assistant to head coach, replacing [[Larry Huras]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.scb.ch/news-content.aspx?id=2498 |title=Coaching change was needed |publisher=www.scb.ch |language=German |date=2011-10-21 |accessdate=2011-11-07 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120403172150/http://www.scb.ch/news-content.aspx?id=2498 |archivedate=2012-04-03 }}</ref> He led SCB to the NLA title in 2013,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.srf.ch/sport/eishockey/nla/sc-bern-ein-meister-der-arbeit|title=SC Bern - ein Meister der Arbeit|last=gaf| website = Schweizer Radio und Fernsehen (SRF)|access-date=2016-03-31}}</ref> but was sacked the following season after a stretch of nine losses in twelve games.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.derbund.ch/sport/hockey/SCB-entlaesst-Antti-Toermaenen/story/29859634|title=SCB entlässt Antti Törmänen| website = derbund.ch/|access-date=2016-03-31}}</ref>

In April 2014, Törmänen agreed to become head coach of Finnish [[Liiga]] side [[HIFK (ice hockey)|HIFK]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.hifk.fi/uutiset/antti-toermaenen-ifkn-paeaevalmentajaksi|title=Uutiset › Antti Törmänen IFK:n päävalmentajaksi - HIFK|website=Hifk|access-date=2016-03-31|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160331222855/http://www.hifk.fi/uutiset/antti-toermaenen-ifkn-paeaevalmentajaksi#|archive-date=2016-03-31|url-status=dead}}</ref> He parted ways with the club at the conclusion of the 2016–17 season. On December 11, 2017, he took over the head coaching job at [[EHC Biel]] in the Swiss [[National League (ice hockey)|National League]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.ehcb.ch/de/news/clubnews/antti-toermaenen-neuer-ehcb-headcoach/|title=Antti Törmänen neuer EHCB Headcoach|work=Antti Törmänen neuer EHCB Headcoach|access-date=2017-12-11|language=de-DE}}</ref>

On 15 March 2020, Törmänen tested positive for [[Coronavirus disease 2019|COVID-19]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tellerreport.com/news/2020-03-15---the-gold-lion--antti-t%C3%B6rm%C3%A4nen--was-identified-as-a-coronavirus-.SyUyy-QnrU.html|title=The gold lion, Antti Törmänen, was identified as a coronavirus|last=Anonym|first=|date=|website=www.tellerreport.com|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-03-16}}</ref> In July 2020 he was diagnosed with [[gallbladder cancer]].<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.is.fi/jaakiekko/art-2000006641525.html| title = MTV Urheilu: Antti Törmänen sairastui sappirakon syöpään – kertoi ensimmäistä kertaa voinnistaan: ”Minulla on ollut kaksi isoa operaatiota”| author = Kivioja, Olli| work = Ilta-Sanomat| date = 19 September 2020| publisher = Sanoma Media Finland Oy| accessdate = 22 September 2020}}</ref>
