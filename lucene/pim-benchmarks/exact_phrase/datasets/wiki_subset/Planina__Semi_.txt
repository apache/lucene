'''Planina''' ({{IPA-sl|plaˈniːna|pron}}; {{lang-de|Stockendorf}},<ref name="Leksikon">''Leksikon občin kraljestev in dežel zastopanih v državnem zboru,'' vol. 6: ''Kranjsko''. 1906. Vienna: C. Kr. Dvorna in Državna Tiskarna, p. 6.</ref><ref>Ferenc, Mitja. 2007. ''Nekdanji nemški jezikovni otok na kočevskem''. Kočevje: Pokrajinski muzej, p. 4.</ref> [[Gottscheerish|Gottschee German]]: ''Aobə''<ref name="Ferenc Zupan">{{cite book |last1=Ferenc |first1=Mitja |last2=Zupan |first2=Gojko |title=Izgubljene kočevske vasi, vol. 1 (A–J). |date=2011 |publisher=Znanstvena založba Filozofske fakultete Univerze v Ljubljani |location=Ljubljana |isbn=9789612374273 |pages=200-206}}</ref>) is a [[village]] in the [[Municipality of Semič]] in [[Slovenia]]. It lies on the southern slopes of [[Mount Mirna]] ({{lang-sl|Mirna gora}}) in the southern part of [[Kočevje Rog]]. It was inhabited by [[Gottschee County|Gottschee Germans]] that were expelled in 1941 during the [[Second World War]]. The area is part of the historical region of [[Lower Carniola]]. The municipality is now included in the [[Southeast Slovenia Statistical Region]].<ref>[http://www.semic.si/ Semič municipal site]</ref> 

==Name==
[[File:Planina Semic Slovenia - gravestone.jpg|thumb|left|[[Gottscheers|Gottschee German]] gravestone with the toponym ''Stockendorf'']]
The name ''Planina'' comes from the Slovene common noun ''planina'', referring to a mountain without trees or to a grassy mountain area used for grazing livestock.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Snoj |first1=Marko |title=Etimološki slovar slovenskih zemljepisnih imen |date=2009 |publisher=Modrijan |location=Ljubljana |page=309}}</ref> The longer name ''Planina pod Mirno Goro'' means 'Planina below [[Mount Mirna]]'. The German name of the settlement, ''Stockendorf'', is a compound of ''Stock'' 'stump' and ''Dorf'' 'village', referring to a settlement at a site where land was cleared by burning and the stumps were then grubbed out.<ref name="Ferenc Zupan"/><ref name="Simonic">{{cite journal |last1=Simonič |first1=Ivan |title=Kočevarji v luči krajevnih in ledinskih imen |journal=Glasnik Muzejskega društva za Slovenijo |date=1935 |volume=16 |pages=64-65 |url=https://www.dlib.si/stream/URN:NBN:SI:DOC-LNG0USOZ/e7ae8251-ea79-4f4e-a78a-8b489dbc21c8/PDF |accessdate=June 28, 2018}}</ref> The [[Gottscheerish|Gottschee German]] name ''Aobə''<ref name="Simonic"/> is the dialect form of the standard German noun ''Alpe'' 'mountain pasture'<ref>{{cite book |last1=Schroer |first1=Karl Julius |title=Wörterbuch der Mundart von Gottschee |date=1870 |publisher=K. k. Hof- und Staatsdruckerei |location=Vienna |page=37}}</ref> and semantically corresponds to the Slovene name ''Planina''.

==Church==
[[File:Planina Semic Slovenia - church 2.jpg|thumb|right|Prophet Elijah Church]]
The local [[parish church]] is dedicated to the [[Elijah|Prophet Elijah]] and belongs to the [[Roman Catholic Diocese of Novo Mesto]]. It was built in around 1730 and extended in the late 18th century. It was renovated in the early 21st century.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://giskd2s.situla.org/rkd/Opis.asp?ESD=2101&submit.x=0&submit.y=0 |title=EŠD 2101 |language=Slovenian |work=Registry of Immovable Cultural Heritage |publisher=Ministry of Culture of the Republic of Slovenia |accessdate=11 March 2011}}</ref>
