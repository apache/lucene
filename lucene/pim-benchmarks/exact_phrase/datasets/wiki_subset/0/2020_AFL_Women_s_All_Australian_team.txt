The '''2020 [[AFL Women's All-Australian team]]''' represents the best-performed players of the [[2020 AFL Women's season]]. It was announced on 27 April 2020 as a complete [[women's Australian rules football]] team of 21 players. The team is honorary and does not play any games.

==Selection panel==
The selection panel for the 2020 AFL Women's All-Australian team consisted of chairwoman [[Nicole Livingstone]], [[Steve Hocking]], Josh Vanderloo, [[Kelli Underwood]], Sarah Black, [[Tim Harrington]], Shelley Ware and [[Sharelle McMahon]].<ref name="Squad">{{cite news|last=Black|first=Sarah|title=REVEALED: Magpies lead the way in All-Australian squad|url=https://womens.afl/news/50080/revealed-magpies-lead-the-way-in-all-australian-squad|work=womens.afl|date=3 April 2020|accessdate=3 April 2020}}</ref><ref name="Statement">{{cite news|title=AFLW statement: 2020 AFLW All-Australian Squad|url=https://womens.afl/news/50079/aflw-statement-2020-aflw-all-australian-squad|work=womens.afl|date=3 April 2020|accessdate=3 April 2020}}</ref>

==Initial squad==
The initial 40-woman All-Australian squad was announced on 3 April. {{AFLW Col}} had the most players selected in the initial squad with five, and every team had at least one representative. Eleven players from the [[2019 AFL Women's All-Australian team|2019 team]] were among those selected.<ref name="Squad"/><ref name="Statement"/><ref>{{cite news|last=Negrepontis|first=Nic|title=Official 2020 AFLW All-Australian squad revealed|url=https://www.sen.com.au/news/2020/04/03/official-2020-aflw-all-australian-squad-revealed/|work=[[1116 SEN|SEN]]|date=3 April 2020|accessdate=3 April 2020}}</ref>

==Final team==
The final team was announced on 27 April. Finalists {{AFLW Car}}, {{AFL Mel}} and {{AFLW NM}} had the most selections with three, and every team except {{AFLW Ric}} and {{AFLW WC}} had at least one representative. Eleven players achieved selection for the first time, while eight players from the 2019 team were selected, two of whom – Melbourne vice-captain [[Karen Paxman]] and North Melbourne captain [[Emma Kearney (sportswoman)|Emma Kearney]] – achieved selection for the fourth consecutive year. Paxman was announced as the All-Australian captain and Kearney was announced as the vice-captain.<ref>{{cite news|last=McGowan|first=Marc|title=AFLW All-Australian team revealed, new wave dominates|url=https://womens.afl/news/50129/aflw-all-australian-team-revealed-new-wave-dominates|work=womens.afl|date=27 April 2020|accessdate=27 April 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Statement: 2020 Toyota AFL Women’s All-Australian Team announced|url=https://womens.afl/news/50134/statement-2020-toyota-afl-women-s-all-australian-team-announced|work=womens.afl|date=27 April 2020|accessdate=28 April 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Laughton|first=Max|title=AFLW All-Australian team for 2020 revealed: 12 clubs represented as two stars make history|url=https://www.foxsports.com.au/afl/womens-afl/aflw-allaustralian-team-for-2020-revealed-12-clubs-represented-as-two-stars-make-history/news-story/7548ceec1657bdbe8cb71abca5c58252|work=[[Fox Sports News (Australia)]]|date=27 April 2020|accessdate=28 April 2020}}</ref>
