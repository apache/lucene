'''Eromo Egbejule''' is a [[Nigeria|Nigerian]] journalist, writer and filmmaker. He is known mostly for his work on the [[Boko Haram]] insurgency<ref>https://www.theguardian.com/world/2016/sep/27/clubbing-birthplace-boko-haram-maiduguri-nigeria</ref><ref>https://www.theguardian.com/world/2016/jan/09/nigeria-baga-massacre-boko-haram-1-year-on</ref><ref>https://www.thenewhumanitarian.org/feature/2016/08/22/they-re-defeating-boko-haram-are-they-nigeria-s-next-security-threat</ref> and other conflicts in [[West Africa|West]] and [[Central Africa]].<ref>https://www.aljazeera.com/features/2018/10/6/death-displacement-trauma-human-cost-of-the-anglophone-crisis</ref><ref>https://www.aljazeera.com/features/2019/9/2/is-another-president-attempting-to-cling-to-power-in-guinea</ref><ref>https://www.reuters.com/article/us-nigeria-violence-aid/nigerias-spiraling-herdsmen-farmer-violence-fuels-fears-of-humanitarian-crisis-idUSKBN15S1KA</ref>

== Background ==
Egbejule was born in [[Sapele, Delta|Sapele]] in the southernmost part of Nigeria.  He has degrees in engineering and media and communications from the [[University of Nigeria, Nsukka]]<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.bellanaija.com/2018/02/award-winning-journalist-storyteller-eromo-egbejule-bellanaijamcm-week/ |title = Award-winning Journalist & Storyteller Eromo Egbejule is our #BellaNaijaMCM this Week|date = 5 February 2018}}</ref> and [[University of Leicester]] respectively.

== Writing career ==
He started as a music journalist, writing for local Nigerian papers like [[The Guardian (Nigeria)|The Guardian]] (Nigeria), [[This Day|ThisDay]],<ref>https://allafrica.com/stories/201502021609.html</ref><ref>https://allafrica.com/stories/201310071437.html</ref> [[Next (Nigeria)|NEXT]] and [[YNaija]].<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://ynaija.com/ynaija-investigation-how-and-why-ghanas-musicians-are-looking-up-to-nigeria-part-1/ | title=YNaija Investigation: How – and why – Ghana's musicians are looking up to Nigeria (Part 1) » YNaija| date=10 June 2013}}</ref> In 2014, he covered the ebola crisis in [[Liberia]] for local Nigerian media, but later that year began working as a freelance reporter and stringer for foreign media on music and culture.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2014/nov/07/nigeria-great-band-names-fela-kuti |title=From Semi Colon to Sweet Breeze: Nigeria's all time great band names &#124; World news |publisher=The Guardian |date= |accessdate=2020-04-03}}</ref> Since then, he has reported extensively on the [[Boko Haram]] insurgency,<ref>{{cite web|author=Eromo Egbejule in Maiduguri |url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2016/sep/27/clubbing-birthplace-boko-haram-maiduguri-nigeria |title=Defiance on the dancefloor: clubbing in the birthplace of Boko Haram &#124; World news |publisher=The Guardian |date= |accessdate=2020-04-03}}</ref> elections across West Africa, sustainability in the Peruvian Amazon, Sino-African relations in the Horn of Africa and other themes.<ref>https://www.theafricareport.com/541/djibouti-small-country-big-stakes/</ref> In a 2017 interview, he is quoted to have said his writing style focuses on 'rotating the cube',<ref>http://wpmu.mah.se/nmict171group8/comdev-lectures/storytelling-journalism-nigeria-eromo-egbejule/</ref> rather than recycling reporting tropes on Africa.

His writing and photography have appeared in [[The Atlantic]],<ref>https://www.theatlantic.com/author/eromo-egbejule/</ref> [[The Guardian]] (UK), [[Al Jazeera English|Al-Jazeera]],<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.aljazeera.com/profile/eromo-egbejule.html |title = Eromo Egbejule &#124; al Jazeera}}</ref>  [[The New York Times|New York Times]], [[Financial Times]], [[The Washington Post|Washington Post]], [[Frankfurter Allgemeine Zeitung]],<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.faz.net/aktuell/gesellschaft/kriminalitaet/benin-die-beute-bronzen-15359996.html |title = Raubkunst aus Benin: Die Beute-Bronzen|newspaper = Faz.net}}</ref> [[Thomson Reuters Foundation]], [[Premium Times]],<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.premiumtimesng.com/news/headlines/242769-special-report-diezani-men-deals-bled-nigeria.html |title = SPECIAL REPORT: How Diezani, her men, their deals bled Nigeria - Premium Times Nigeria|date = 9 September 2017}}</ref> [[Telegraph (UK)|Telegraph]] (UK),<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/authors/eromo-egbejule/ | title=Eromo Egbejule}}</ref> [[The Times]]<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/nigeria-reclaims-the-champion-it-rejected-6dv0g6fnt |title = Nigeria reclaims the champion it 'rejected'|last1 = Lagos|first1 = Eromo Egbejule}}</ref> and more. In 2020, he joined [[OZY Media|OZY]] as its Africa Editor,<ref>{{cite web|author=Estimated Reading Time: <1 |url=https://newswirengr.com/2020/01/02/nigerian-journalist-eromo-egbejule-joins-ozy-magazine-as-the-first-african-editor/ |title=Nigerian journalist, Eromo Egbejule joins OZY Magazine as the First African Editor |publisher=NewsWireNGR |date=2020-01-02 |accessdate=2020-04-03}}</ref> just months after leaving his role as West Africa Editor for ''[[The Africa Report]]'' magazine (2018-2019).

In fall 2019, he was named one of four [[Dag Hammarskjöld|Dag Hammarsjköld]] Journalism Fellows at the [[United Nations Headquarters]] in New York for his work in covering 'husband schools' in rural [[Sierra Leone]], setup to combat gender-based violence in the country.<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-leone-women-abuse/no-more-beatings-sierra-leones-husband-schools-take-on-domestic-violence-idUSKBN1CE08X |title = 'No more beatings': Sierra Leone's husband schools take on domestic violence|newspaper = Reuters|date = 9 October 2017}}</ref> His narrative nonfiction has also been shortlisted for the 2019 [[Miles Morland Foundation Writing Scholarship]] for narrative nonfiction. He has been described as "one of the country’s most important storytellers".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ynaija.com/eromo-egbejule-is-partnering-with-arit-okpo-to-revisit-the-tragedy-of-jesse/|title=Eromo Egbejule is partnering with Arit Okpo to revisit the tragedy of 'Jesse' » YNaija|date=23 October 2019}}</ref>

Egbejule has also made intermittent incursions into academics, having been a visiting lecturer and researcher to [[Malmö University]],<ref>{{cite web|author=Tobias Denskus |url=http://wpmu.mah.se/comdev/2017/03/07/storytelling-journalism-nigeria-eromo-egbejule/ |title=Contemporary storytelling from Nigeria with ComDev visiting lecturer Eromo Egbejule - COMMUNICATION FOR DEVELOPMENT PORTAL |publisher=Wpmu.mah.se |date=2017-03-07 |accessdate=2020-04-03}}</ref> [[Sweden]] across February 2017. He has also taught lectures and seminar classes at the [[University of Copenhagen]],<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://teol.ku.dk/cas/publications/newsletters/cas_enews/?newsletter=6428 |title = Electronic newsletter CAS eNews|date = 19 September 2011}}</ref> [[Linnaeus University]], [[Växjö]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://artsandafrica.com/an-interview-with-eromo-egbejule-a3561e9a782f |title=An Interview with Eromo Egbejule |publisher=Arts and Africa |date= 22 September 2017|accessdate=2020-04-03}}</ref> and [[New York University]] on among other things, his coverage of the insurgency in the [[Sahel]] and [[Anglophone Crisis|Anglophone crisis]] in [[Cameroon]]. In 2014, he was a recipient of the Prince Claus travel grant <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pulse.ng/lifestyle/food-travel/mcm-journalist-and-storyteller-eromo-egbejule-is-our-man-crush-this-monday/v75fqcg |title=#MCM Journalist and storyteller, Eromo Egbejule, is our Man Crush this Monday! - Pulse Nigeria |publisher=Pulse.ng |date= 17 September 2018|accessdate=2020-04-03}}</ref> for culture and development, to facilitate a short teaching spell in Mexico.
