'''4043 aluminium alloy'''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.aircraftmaterials.com/data/weld/4043.html|title=Alloy 4043/ Aluminium Welding Alloy 4043|website=www.aircraftmaterials.com|access-date=2019-05-18}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.azom.com/article.aspx?ArticleID=8685|title=Aluminum 4043 Alloy (UNS A94043)|date=2013-05-02|website=AZoM.com|language=en|access-date=2019-05-18}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.harrisproductsgroup.com/en/Products/Alloys/Welding/Aluminum-Alloy/Alloy-4043-MIG.aspx|title=Alloy 4043 (MIG) {{!}} The Harris Products Group|website=www.harrisproductsgroup.com|language=en|access-date=2019-05-18}}</ref> is a wrought aluminium alloy with good corrosion resistance typically used as filler material<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.researchgate.net/publication/330227953|title=Characterisation of 4043 aluminium alloy deposits obtained by wire and arc additive manufacturing using a Cold Metal Transfer process {{!}} Request PDF|website=ResearchGate|language=en|access-date=2019-05-18}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.efunda.com/Materials/alloys/aluminum/show_aluminum.cfm?ID=AA_4043&show_prop=all&Page_Title=AA%204043|title=eFunda: Properties of Aluminum Alloy AA 4043|website=www.efunda.com|access-date=2019-05-18}}</ref> for welding of aluminium parts. It contains high amounts of Silicon (between 4.5 and 6.0%) and trace amounts of other metals.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefabricator.com/article/aluminumwelding/aluminum-workshop-using-4043-versus-4047-filler-wire|title=Aluminum Workshop: Using 4043 versus 4047 filler wire|website=www.thefabricator.com|language=en|access-date=2019-05-18}}</ref> It is grey in appearance and is sold as either welding wire or welding rod for [[Gas tungsten arc welding|TIG]]/[[Gas metal arc welding|MIG]] processes.
