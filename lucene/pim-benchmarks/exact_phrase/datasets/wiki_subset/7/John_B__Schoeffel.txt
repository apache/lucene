'''John Baptist Schoeffel''' (11 May 1846 - d. [[Boston]], 31 August 1918),<ref name="NYT_31_Aug_1918">{{cite news |newspaper=New York Times |date=31 August 1918 |title=John B. Schoeffel dies in Boston at 72 |url=https://timesmachine.nytimes.com/timesmachine/1918/09/01/97023447.pdf |accessdate=12 May 2017}}</ref> was an American theatre manager and producer, and hotel owner. With [[Henry Eugene Abbey|Henry E. Abbey]] he was involved presenting European theatrical stars in the US, including [[Sarah Bernhardt]], [[Henry Irving]] and [[Ellen Terry]]: and with [[Maurice Grau]]<ref name=Sun_Obit >{{cite news |title=Impresario Grau Is Dead |newspaper=The Sun |place=New York City |date=15 March 1907|page=9a |url=http://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn83030272/1907-03-15/ed-1/seq-9/}}</ref> he and Abbey managed opera singers as [[Adelina Patti]], [[Christina Nilsson]], [[Ernestine Schumann-Heink]], [[Francesco Tamagno]] and [[Fyodor Chaliapin]] in their tours of opera houses in Boston, Chicago and New York.

==Career==
{{main|Abbey, Schoeffel and Grau}}
He was born in [[Rochester, NY]].<ref name="NYT_31_Aug_1918" /> He joined the theatre impresario [[Henry Eugene Abbey|Henry E. Abbey]] as his business partner in 1876. As a partner in the firm of Abbey, Schoeffel and Grau formed in 1880, he was involved in presenting grand opera during the 'Golden Age of singing' at the New York [[Metropolitan Opera House (39th Street)|Metropolitan Opera House]] ("the old Met") in 1883 and from 1891-1903.

He was resident Manager of the [[Park Theatre (Boston)|Park Theatre]], [[Boston]] when it was built in 1879, and manager of the 1889 [[Tremont Theatre, Boston (1889)|Tremont Theatre, Boston]] until his death.<ref name="NYT_31_Aug_1918" />

Abbey and Schoeffel managed the 10-year old [[Josef Hofmann]] when he toured the USA, but he was stopped from playing because of overwork. A letter from Schoeffel from the Tremont Theatre dates from this period.<ref>{{cite book |title=Autograph letter signed from John B. Schoeffel, Boston, to unidentified recipient, 4 September 1888 |url=http://www.worldcat.org/title/autograph-letter-signed-from-john-b-schoeffel-boston-to-unidentified-recipient-1888-september-4/oclc/319887020 |website=Worldcat |oclc=319887020 |accessdate=23 April 2017}}
Schoeffel indicates that they are not currently making dates for Master Hofmann, presumably Josef Hofmann, who was represented by Schoeffel and Abbey. Addressed "Dear Sir" and signed Jno. B. Schoeffel. He produced some plays at [[Daly's Theatre]] on Broadway in 1904 after Grau retired. One of these, [[Henrik Ibsen]]'s ''[[Hedda Gabler]]'', starred [[Nance O'Neill]], a close friend of [[Lizzie Borden]].<ref>[https://www.ibdb.com/broadway-cast-staff/john-b-schoeffel-22635 "John B. ==Personal life==
In 1885 he married the Australian actress [[Agnes Booth]] (née Marion Agnes Land Rookes) (4 October 1841? - 2 January 1910), the widow of [[Junius Brutus Booth Jr.]], (brother of [[John Wilkes Booth]] and [[Edwin Booth]], owner of [[Booth's Theatre]]) as her second husband.{{sfn|James|James|Boyer|1971|pp=202-3}}

Together they managed the huge Masconomo hotel in [[Manchester-by-the-Sea, Massachusetts]] until her death in 1910. He purchased the property outright at public auction in 1911, which changed hands before its complete destruction by fire in 1919.{{sfn|Tolles|2008|pp=98-9}}

==Death==
Schoeffel died at [[Massachusetts General Hospital]], Boston, MA,  on 31 August 1918 after a stroke two weeks earlier.<ref name="NYT_31_Aug_1918" /> He was buried at the [[Mount Hope Cemetery, Rochester]], NY.
