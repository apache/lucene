The '''2017 Maccabiah Games''' ({{lang-he|משחקי המכביה 2017}}), also referred to as the  '''20th Maccabiah Games''' ({{lang-he|המכביה ה-20}}), were the 20th edition of the [[Maccabiah Games]]. They took place from 4 to 17 July 2017, in Israel. The Maccabiah Games are open to [[Jews in Sports|Jewish athletes]] from around the world, and to all [[Israeli citizen]]s regardless of their religion. A total of 10,000 athletes competed, a Maccabiah Games record, making the 2017 Maccabiah Games the third-largest sporting competition in the world (after the [[Olympics]] and the [[FIFA World Cup]]).<ref name="NY"/><ref name=autogenerated5>[http://www.ottawasun.com/2017/06/11/levine-inducted-into-jewish-sports-hall-as-maccabiah-athletes-feted-at-jc "Levine inducted into Jewish sports hall as Maccabiah athletes feted at JC,"<!-- Bot generated title -->] ''Ottawa Sun''.</ref> The athletes were from 85 countries, also a record.<ref name="NY"/> Countries represented for the first time included the Bahamas, Barbados, Cambodia, the Cayman Islands, Haiti, Malta, Morocco, the Philippines, Singapore, South Korea, and Trinidad (according to the official Games website Morocco debuted in 2013, and it does not list The Bahamas, Cambodia, Haiti, Malta, the Philippines, or Trinidad among participating nations).  The athletes competed in 45 sports.

==History==
The Maccabiah Games were first held in [[1932 Maccabiah Games|1932]].<ref>[http://www.cjnews.com/perspectives/20th-maccabiah-games-pt-1 "The 20th Maccabiah Games: A brief History (Part 1),"<!-- Bot generated title -->] ''The Canadian Jewish News''.</ref> In 1961, they were declared a "Regional Sports Event" by, and under the auspices and supervision of, the [[International Olympic Committee]].<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=nTHRB6Fj_4gC&q=maccabiah+games+olympics+regional+sport+event&pg=PA56|title=Gender Politics and the Olympic Industry|author=Helen Jefferson Lenskyj|publisher=Palgrave Macmillan|year=2012|isbn=9781137291158}}</ref><ref name=bard>Mitchell G. Bard and Moshe Schwartz (2005). [https://books.google.com/books?id=PeJFAAAAQBAJ&pg=PA85&dq=Bard+and+Schwartz+maccabiah&hl=en&sa=X&ved=0ahUKEwjuxs2ai_DUAhWEaz4KHRmGD4YQ6AEIKDAA#v=onepage&q=Bard%20and%20Schwartz%20maccabiah&f=false ''1001 Facts Everyone Should Know about Israel''] p. 84.</ref><ref name=MaccabiAu>{{cite web|title=History of the Maccabiah Games|url=http://www.maccabi.com.au/Maccabiah/Page/1591/Maccabiah-Games-History/History-of-the-Maccabiah-Games.cfm|publisher=Maccabi Australia|accessdate=}}</ref>

==Opening ceremonies==
On 6 July 2017, the opening ceremonies took place at [[Teddy Stadium]] in Jerusalem, with 10,000 competitors in front of 30,000 spectators.<ref>[http://reshet.tv/item/general/branded/maccabi-games-2017-355329/ "המכביה ה-20 טקס הפתיחה", film of opening ceremony], reshet.tv.</ref><ref name=autogenerated2>[http://www.haaretz.com/israel-news/travel/1.798377 "Maccabiah Games: Your guide to celebrating the 'Jewish Olympics' in Israel; Maccabiah Games activities: A star-studded opening ceremony, a night run and parties, parties and more parties,"<!-- Bot generated title -->] ''Haaretz''.</ref><ref>[https://www.thejc.com/news/israel/maccabiah-games-opens-in-style-in-jerusalem-1.441132 "Maccabiah Games opens in style in Jerusalem; A record 10,000 athletes danced and sang in a lavish ceremony in front of 30,000 spectators,"] ''The Jewish Chronicle''.</ref>  Seven athletes took part in the torch lighting ceremony, including Olympic gold-medal-winner swimmers American [[Anthony Ervin]] and Frenchman [[Fabien Gilot]].<ref name=autogenerated3>[https://swimswam.com/ervin-gilot-participate-torch-lighting-maccabiah-games/ "Ervin, Gilot To Participate In Torch Lighting At Maccabiah Games,"<!-- Bot generated title -->] ''SwimSwam Magazine''.</ref> Five Israelis participated in the torch lighting ceremony:  Olympic medalists in judo [[Ori Sasson]] and [[Yarden Gerbi]], three-time Olympic gymnast [[Neta Rivkin]], [[National Basketball Association]] player [[Omri Casspi]], and Paralympic world champion rower [[Moran Samuel]].<ref name=autogenerated3 /><ref name=autogenerated9>[http://www.timesofisrael.com/maccabiah-2017-opens-with-a-high-energy-ceremony-and-a-surprise-wedding/ "Watch: Maccabiah 2017 opens with a high-energy ceremony - and a surprise wedding,"<!-- Bot generated title -->] ''The Times of Israel.''</ref>

The delegation march took place before Israeli President [[Reuven Rivlin]], Prime Minister [[Benjamin Netanyahu]], and others.<ref name=autogenerated2 /><ref name=autogenerated9 /> British Prime Minister [[Theresa May]] and Canadian Prime Minister [[Justin Trudeau]] spoke to the crowd by means of pre-recorded videos.<ref>[http://www.jweekly.com/2017/07/06/maccabiah-games-get-underway-with-huge-opening-ceremony-in-jerusalem/ "Maccabiah Games get underway with huge opening ceremony in Jerusalem,"<!-- Bot generated title -->] ''J''.</ref>

Canadian ice hockey player Avi Steinberg proposed to his girlfriend Rachel on stage, and then married her under a ''[[chuppah]]'' on stage minutes later.<ref name=stage/> Singers who performed included [[Lior Narkis]], [[Ester Rada]], [[Omer Adam]], [[Victoria Hanna]], and [[Marina Maximilian Blumin|Marina Maximillian]].<ref name=autogenerated2 /><ref name=stage>[http://www.jpost.com/Diaspora/Maccabiah-Marriage-A-surprise-proposal-turned-surprise-wedding-499035 "Maccabiah marriage: A surprise proposal turned surprise wedding,"<!-- Bot generated title -->] ''The Jerusalem Post''.</ref>

==Notable medalists==

J. Johnson]] won gold medals in the men's 100m [[breaststroke]], with a time of 1:01.27 (a new Maccabiah record), as well as in the 200m breaststroke, with a time of 2:11.60.<ref name=bj>[https://swimswam.com/ervin-opens-maccabiah-games-100-free-gold/ "Ervin Opens Maccabiah Games With 100 Free Gold,"<!-- Bot generated title -->] ''SwimSwam Magazine''.</ref><ref>[http://www.jpost.com/Israel-News/Sports/Records-fall-as-Olympians-shine-at-Maccabiah-Games-499449 "Records fall as Olympians shine at Maccabiah Games; Olympic champion Anthony Ervin makes a splash at the 20th Games,"] ''The Jerusalem Post''.</ref> In the special 4x50m relay race between Israeli and American all-star teams, American Olympic champions [[Lenny Krayzelburg]] (four Olympic golds), [[Jason Lezak]] (four Olympic golds), and Ervin, with masters swimmer Alex Blavatnik, swam a time of 1:48.23 and defeated Israeli Olympians [[Guy Barnea]], [[Yoav Bruck]], [[Eran Groumi]], and [[Tal Stricker]], who had a time of 1:51.25.<ref>[http://www.jpost.com/Israel-News/Sports/Records-fall-as-Olympians-shine-at-Maccabiah-Games-499449 "Records fall as Olympians shine at Maccabiah Games,"<!-- Bot generated title -->] ''The Jerusalem Post''.</ref> American [[Jessica Antiles]] won the silver medal in the 400m individual medley and a bronze medal in the 200m individual medley.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.gocolumbialions.com/ViewArticle.dbml?DB_OEM_ID=9600&ATCLID=211644356|title=Jessica Antiles Medals In 200 IM & 400 IM At Maccabiah Games|work=gocolumbialions.com The Official Athletics Website of Columbia University in the City of New York|access-date=2017-10-27}}</ref> American [[Sam Raben]] was part of Team USA's gold medal winnings soccer team. ==Participating countries==
The Maccabiah Games are open to [[Jews in Sports|Jewish athletes]] from around the world, and to all [[Israeli citizen]]s regardless of their religion.<ref>[http://www.cjnews.com/perspectives/the-maccabiah-games-part-2-competition-and-controversy "Jewish Games not loved by all: Competition and Controversy,"<!-- Bot generated title -->] ''The Canadian Jewish News''.</ref> Some delegations have more relaxed rules about participation; for example, the Brazil junior football team included Ronald, the son of former three-time [[FIFA World Player of the Year]] [[Ronaldo (Brazilian footballer)|Ronaldo]], who is not Jewish, but has been a member of the Jewish football club Hebraica for years.<ref>[http://www.timesofisrael.com/soccer-legend-ronaldos-son-to-play-in-israels-maccabiah-games/ "Soccer legend Ronaldo’s son to play in Maccabiah,"<!-- Bot generated title -->] ''The Times of Israel''.</ref>

The athletes were from a record 85 countries.<ref name="NY"/><ref name=autogenerated7 /> Countries represented for the first time include the Bahamas, Cambodia, the Cayman Islands, Haiti, Malta, Morocco, the Philippines, Singapore, South Korea, and Trinidad (according to the official Web site of the Games, Morocco debuted in 2013, official Web site of the Games does not list Malta or Trinidad among participating nations)<ref name="List of Countries"/><ref name="NY" /> A total of 10,000 athletes competed, a Maccabiah Games record, making the 2017 Maccabiah Games the third-largest sporting competition in the world (after the [[Olympics]] and the [[FIFA World Cup]]).<ref name="NY"/><ref name=autogenerated5 />

Israel fielded the largest delegation, with 2,500 competitors led by Olympic windsurfer [[Maayan Davidovich]].<ref name=autogenerated4>[http://www.heritagefl.com/story/2017/06/30/news/international-jewish-athletes-return-to-jerusalem-for-20th-maccabiah-games/8175.html "International Jewish athletes 'return' to Jerusalem for 20th Maccabiah Games,"<!-- Bot generated title -->] ''Heritage Florida Jewish News''.</ref><ref name=autogenerated11>[https://www.ynetnews.com/articles/0,7340,L-4986047,00.html "Canadian ice hockey player gets married at Maccabiah Games opening ceremony,"], Ynet.</ref>  The US delegation of over 1,100 athletes came from over 42 states, and was the second-largest delegation.<ref name=autogenerated1>[https://www.maccabiusa.com/maccabi-usa-to-bring-largest-sports-delegation-ever-to-world-maccabiah-games/ "Maccabi USA To Bring Largest Sports Delegation Ever To World Maccabiah Games,"<!-- Bot generated title -->] Maccabi USA.</ref> For Team USA, Olympians [[Eli Dershwitz]] (fencing), Anthony Ervin (swimming), and [[Zach Test]] (rugby 7's) competed, and Olympic gold medalists and Maccabiah Games alumni [[Lenny Krayzelburg]] and [[Jason Lezak]] joined the team as Maccabi Ambassadors, and swam two exhibition relays.<ref name=autogenerated1 /><ref>[https://www.swimmingworldmagazine.com/news/lenny-krayzelburg-and-jason-lezak-join-usa-contingent-for-maccabiah-games/ "Lenny Krayzelburg & Jason Lezak Join US Contingent for Maccabiah Games,"<!-- Bot generated title -->] ''Swimming World Magazine''.</ref> The Argentinian, Australian, and Canadian delegations each had approximately 650 athletes, Brazil's delegation had 491 members, and Great Britain was represented by 375 competitors.<ref>[https://www.thejc.com/sport/maccabiah/stars-of-david-the-history-of-maccabiah-1.440725 "Stars of David: The history of Maccabiah,"] ''The Jewish Chronicle''.</ref><ref name=autogenerated11 />

==Medal count==
Israel won the most medals, a total of 470 (173 gold medals, 150 silver, and 147 bronze).<ref name=autogenerated18>[http://www.baltimoresun.com/sports/bs-sp-marylanders-maccabiah-games-0718-story.html "Marylanders earn medals at 20th Maccabiah Games,"<!-- Bot generated title -->] ''Baltimore Sun''.</ref><ref name=autogenerated17>[http://www.maccabiah.com/2017/the-games/medals "Medals - 20th Maccabiah 2017,"<!-- Bot generated title -->] Maccabiah.com.</ref>  Team USA finished second with 164 medals (50 gold, 67 silver, and 47 bronze).<ref name=autogenerated18 /><ref name=autogenerated17 />  Australia came in third (40; 9 gold, 19 silver, and 12 bronze), and Canada came in fourth (23; 10 gold, 6 silver, and 7 bronze).<ref name=autogenerated17 />  Hungary took fifth with 17 medals (6 gold, 7 silver, and 4 bronze).<ref name=autogenerated17 />

South Africa came in sixth with 15 medals (9 gold, 1 silver, and 5 bronze).<ref name=autogenerated17 /> Tied for seventh with 14 medals were Germany (7 gold, 1 silver, and 6 bronze), Great Britain (4 gold, 5 silver, and 5 bronze), and Russia (1 gold, 2 silver, and 11 bronze).<ref name=autogenerated17 /> Ukraine came in 10th with 13 medals (3 gold, 8 silver, and 2 bronze), followed by France with 12 (3 gold, 5 silver, and 4 bronze).<ref name=autogenerated17 />

==Closing ceremony==
The closing ceremony in [[Latrun]], between [[Tel Aviv]] and [[Jerusalem]], on July 17, 2017, was hosted by [[Bar Refaeli]].<ref>[http://www.jpost.com/Israel-News/Sports/IN-PICTURES-Maccabiah-ends-with-spectacular-ceremony-500009 "In Pictures: Maccabiah ends with spectacular ceremony,"<!-- Bot generated title -->] ''The Jerusalem Post''.</ref><ref name=autogenerated15>[http://www.jpost.com/Israel-News/Sports/20th-Maccabiah-Games-Opens-With-Spectacular-Ceremony-at-Teddy-Stadium-498976 "20th Maccabiah Games open with spectacular Jerusalem ceremony,"<!-- Bot generated title -->] ''The Jerusalem Post''.</ref><ref name=autogenerated16>[http://www.jpost.com/Israel-News/Sports/Athletes-make-last-medal-pushes-499824 "Athletes make last medal pushes,"<!-- Bot generated title -->] ''The Jerusalem Post''.</ref> Refaeli presented Canadian Pan American Games medalist [[Sasha Gollish]], who won three gold medals in track, with the Female Athlete of the Games Award.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cjnews.com/culture/sports/canada-wraps-maccabiah-games-50-medals|title=Canada wraps up Maccabiah games with 50 medals|date=21 July 2017|publisher=|accessdate=26 August 2018}}</ref> Israeli [[Ministry of Culture and Sport (Israel)|Minister of Culture and Sport]] [[Miri Regev]] passed the Maccabiah torch to a number of Maccabiah athletes.<ref name=autogenerated15 /><ref name=autogenerated16 />
