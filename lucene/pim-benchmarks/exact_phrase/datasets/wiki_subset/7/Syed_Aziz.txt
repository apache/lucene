'''Syed Aziz''' (born 7 October 1998) is a Malaysian [[cricket]]er.<ref name="Bio">{{Cite web|url=http://www.espncricinfo.com/ci/content/player/868003.html |title=Syed Aziz |accessdate=23 May 2017 |work=ESPN Cricinfo}}</ref> He played for [[Malaysia national cricket team|Malaysia]] in the [[2017 ICC World Cricket League Division Three]] tournament in May 2017.<ref name="Div3">{{Cite web|url=http://www.espncricinfo.com/ci/engine/match/1090938.html |title=ICC World Cricket League Division Three, Malaysia v Singapore at Kampala, May 23, 2017 |accessdate=23 May 2017 |work=ESPN Cricinfo}}</ref> In April 2018, he was named in Malaysia's squad for the [[2018 ICC World Cricket League Division Four]] tournament, also in Malaysia.<ref name="MasSquad">{{cite web|url=https://www.icc-cricket.com/media-releases/673100 |title=Six teams travel to Malaysia on road to ICC Cricket World Cup 2023 |date=28 April 2018 |accessdate=28 April 2018 |work=International Cricket Council}}</ref>

In August 2018, he was named in Malaysia's squad for the [[2018 Asia Cup Qualifier]] tournament.<ref name="MasACQ">{{cite web|url=http://www.asiancricket.org/index.php/tournaments/acc-asia-cup-qualifier-2018/3272 |title=Malaysia Team |work=Asian Cricket Council |accessdate=23 August 2018}}</ref><ref name="MasSquad2">{{cite web|url=https://www.cricketmalaysia.com/malaysia-put-seasoned-players-forward-for-acc-asia-cup-qualifiers-2018/ |title=Malaysia put seasoned players forward for ACC Asia Cup Qualifiers 2018 |work=Cricket Malaysia |accessdate=23 August 2018}}</ref> In October 2018, he was named in Malaysia's squad in the Eastern sub-region group for the [[2018–19 ICC World Twenty20 Asia Qualifier]] tournament.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.icc-cricket.com/media-releases/869213 |title=Exciting battle on the cards in the ICC World T20 Asia Qualifier B in Malaysia |work=International Cricket Council |accessdate=2 October 2018}}</ref>

In December 2018, he was one of two Malaysian cricketers signed to play a four-month stint of [[first-class cricket]] in Sri Lanka.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sportimes.my/malaysian-duo-set-for-first-class-cricket-in-sri-lanka/ |title=Malaysian duo set for first class cricket in Sri Lanka |work= Sportimes |accessdate=12 February 2019}}</ref> He made his first-class debut for [[Sri Lanka Police Sports Club (cricket)|Sri Lanka Police Sports Club]] in Tier B of the [[2018–19 Premier League Tournament]] on 11 February 2019.<ref name="FC">{{Cite web|url=http://www.espncricinfo.com/ci/engine/match/1173752.html |title=12th Match, Premier League Tournament Tier B at Colombo, Feb 11-13 2019 |accessdate=12 February 2019 |work=ESPN Cricinfo}}</ref>

In June 2019, he was named in Malaysia's squad for the [[2019 Malaysia Tri-Nation Series]] tournament.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://api.cricclubs.com/MalaysianCricketAssociation/viewTeam.do?teamId=1766&clubId=10933 |title=Malaysia (MCA T20i Tri-Series 2019) |work=CricClubs |accessdate=22 June 2019}}</ref> He made his [[Twenty20 International]] (T20I) debut for Malayasia, against [[Thailand national cricket team|Thailand]], on 24 June 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espncricinfo.com/ci/engine/match/1186488.html |title=1st Match, Malaysia Tri-Nation T20I Series at Kuala Lumpur, Jun 24 2019 |work=ESPN Cricinfo |accessdate=24 June 2019}}</ref> In September 2019, he was named in Malaysia's squad for the [[2019 Malaysia Cricket World Cup Challenge League A]] tournament.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cricketmalaysia.com/MalaysianCricketAssociation/viewTeam.do?teamId=1926&clubId=10933 |title=ICC CWC Challenge League A 2019: Team List |work=Malaysian Cricket Association |accessdate=12 September 2019}}</ref> He made his [[List A cricket|List A]] debut for Malaysia, against [[Denmark national cricket team|Denmark]], in the Cricket World Cup Challenge League A tournament on 16 September 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espncricinfo.com/ci/engine/match/1200438.html |title=1st Match, CWC Challenge League Group A at Kuala Lumpur, Sep 16 2019 |work=ESPN Cricinfo |accessdate=16 September 2019}}</ref>
