'''Menlo Castle''' also called '''Blake's Castle''' (1569–1910), is a 16th Century castle situated on the bank of the [[River Corrib]] in [[County Galway]]. It was built in 1569 and was home to the [[Blake_baronets#Blake_baronets,_of_Menlough_(1622)|Blake family]]. The ruins still exist today close to the [[National University of Ireland]] (NUI) campus.

==History==

On 26 July 1910, while Sir Valentine Blake (14th baronet) and Lady Blake were away in Dublin, a fire broke out In the castle.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Kenny|first=Tom|date=2009|title=The Fire at Menlo Castle|url=https://www.advertiser.ie/galway/article/14416/the-fire-at-menlo-castle|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-09-24|website=Galway Advertiser}}</ref> Their disabled daughter Eleanor’s body was never found, the castle was destroyed and she presumably died in the fire.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Abandoned Menlo Castle|url=http://www.atlasobscura.com/places/abandoned-menlo-castle|access-date=2020-08-31|website=Atlas Obscura|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Menlo Castle, Co. Galway|url=http://www.abandonedireland.com/mnl.html|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-08-31|website=Abandoned Ireland}}</ref>

Sir Ulick Blake (16th baronet) inherited the ruin. He was found dead in his car some years later.
