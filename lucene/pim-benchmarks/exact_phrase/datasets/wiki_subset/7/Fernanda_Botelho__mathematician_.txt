'''Fernanda Maria Botelho''' (born 1957)<ref>Birth year from [https://www.idref.fr/203584686 idRef authority control file], retrieved 2018-12-03.</ref> is an American mathematician, a professor and the director of graduate studies and coordinator of mathematics at the [[University of Memphis]].<ref>[http://www.memphis.edu/msci/people/mbotelho.php Faculty profile], University of Memphis, retrieved 2017-05-22.</ref> Botelho earned her Ph.D. in 1988 at the [[University of California at Berkeley]], where [[Jenny Harrison]] was her doctoral advisor.<ref>{{MathGenealogy|id=31808}}</ref> Earlier she did her M.Sc in 1985 and B.Sc in 1981 in mathematics at the [[Universidade do Porto]]. Her research interests include [[functional analysis]], [[operator theory]] and [[dynamical system]]s. From 2013 through 2016, she held the Dunavant Professorship at the University of Memphis.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://umwa.memphis.edu/fcv/viewcv.php?personUUID=mbotelho|website=University of Memphis|title=CV for Fernanda Botelho|access-date=2013-03-03}}</ref>
