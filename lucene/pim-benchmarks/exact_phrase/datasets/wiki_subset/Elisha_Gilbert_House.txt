'''Elisha Gilbert House''' is a historic home located at [[New Lebanon, New York|New Lebanon]] in [[Columbia County, New York]].  Built in 1794, the home is a massive, two story frame [[Federal architecture|Federal]] style residence with a gambrel roof and a five bay facade with a center entrance pavilion and clapboard siding.  The attic level once held a [[Masonic Lodge]] meeting hall.  Also on the property is a family [[cemetery]] and 19th century barn.<ref name="nrhpinv_ny">{{cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=1436|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: Elisha Gilbert House|date=July 1984|accessdate=2010-07-03 |author=Neil G. Larson|publisher=[[New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation]]}}</ref>

It was added to the [[National Register of Historic Places]] in 1984.<ref name="nris"/>
