[[Landon Donovan]] is a professional [[association football|soccer]] player who played for the [[United States men's national soccer team]] from 2000 to 2014. In his 157 appearances for the United States, he scored 57 goals, making him the country's [[List of top international association football goal scorers by country|all-time male top scorer]].<ref name="RSSSF">{{cite web |last=Mamrud |first=Roberto |date=October 30, 2014 |title=Landon Timothy Donovan – Century of International Appearances |url=http://www.rsssf.com/miscellaneous/donovan-intlg.html |publisher=[[Rec.Sport.Soccer Statistics Foundation]] |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160401064228/http://www.rsssf.com/miscellaneous/donovan-intlg.html |archivedate=April 1, 2016 |accessdate=June 21, 2016}}</ref><ref name="USSF">{{cite web |date=October 7, 2014 |title=#LegenD: Landon Donovan by the numbers |url=http://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2014/10/07/10/18/141007-landon-donovan-by-numbers |publisher=[[United States Soccer Federation]] |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20161201211855/http://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2014/10/07/10/18/141007-landon-donovan-by-numbers/ |archivedate=December 1, 2016 |accessdate=June 21, 2016}}</ref> Since July 22, 2017, Donovan has been tied with [[Clint Dempsey]] as the all-time male top scorer.<ref>{{cite news |last=Goff |first=Steven |date=July 23, 2017 |title=Clint Dempsey leads U.S. to Gold Cup semifinal win over Costa Rica |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/soccer-insider/wp/2017/07/23/clint-dempsey-leads-u-s-to-gold-cup-semifinal-win-over-costa-rica/ |work=[[The Washington Post]] |accessdate=July 23, 2017}}</ref>

Donovan scored in his debut for the United States, a 2–0 win over [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]] in a [[Exhibition game|friendly]] played on October 25, 2000. His first competitive goal came on January 19, 2002, against [[South Korea national football team|South Korea]] in the [[2002 CONCACAF Gold Cup]]. A few months later, he played in his first [[FIFA World Cup]], scoring against [[Poland national football team|Poland]] on June 14, 2002, and Mexico on June 17, 2002.<ref>{{cite news |last=Arena |first=Bruce |authorlink=Bruce Arena |date=October 7, 2014 |title=#LegenD: Bruce Arena recalls Donovan's breakout at the 2002 World Cup |url=http://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2014/10/07/18/01/141007-ld-bruce-arena-on-02wc |publisher=United States Soccer Federation |accessdate=June 21, 2016}}</ref> During the [[2006 FIFA World Cup qualification]] campaign, Donovan scored six goals to help the United States qualify for the [[2006 FIFA World Cup]], where he did not score.<ref>{{cite news |last=Oberjuerge |first=Paul |date=March 27, 2009 |title=Love him or hate him, at least sports fans know the name Landon Donovan |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/03/28/sports/soccer/28soccer.html?_r=0 |newspaper=[[The New York Times]] |accessdate=June 21, 2016}}</ref>

On January 20, 2008, Donovan scored his 35th goal, against [[Sweden national football team|Sweden]], surpassing [[Eric Wynalda]] to become the [[United States men's national soccer team player statistics|United States's all-time male top goalscorer]].<ref name="UA2008SWE">{{cite news |date=January 20, 2008 |title=Donovan sets record in Team USA win over Sweden |url=http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/sports/soccer/2008-01-20-men-usa-sweden_N.htm |newspaper=[[USA Today]] |agency=[[Associated Press]] |accessdate=June 21, 2016}}</ref> At the [[2010 FIFA World Cup]], Donovan scored three goals, against [[Slovenia national football team|Slovenia]] and [[Algeria national football team|Algeria]] in the group stage and [[Ghana national football team|Ghana]] in the Round of 16. His goal against Algeria, which took the United States into the knockout round, was named the second most significant goal in United States history by ''[[Sports Illustrated]]'' in May 2014.<ref>{{cite news |last=Strauss |first=Brian |date=May 20, 2014 |title=The 10 most significant goals in U.S. soccer history: Landon Donovan |url=https://www.si.com/longform/soccer-goals/goal2.html |work=[[Sports Illustrated]] |accessdate=June 21, 2016}}</ref> Donovan scored five goals for the United States during their victory at the [[2013 CONCACAF Gold Cup]], sharing the top goalscorer award with American teammate [[Chris Wondolowski]] and Panamanian striker [[Gabriel Torres]].<ref>{{cite news|date=July 28, 2013 |title=Gold Cup: Landon Donovan, Chris Wondolowski & Gabriel Torres share Golden Boot |url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2013/07/28/gold-cup-landon-donovan-chris-wondolowski-gabriel-torres-share-golden-boot |publisher=MLSsoccer.com |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160817010038/http://www.mlssoccer.com/post/2013/07/28/gold-cup-landon-donovan-chris-wondolowski-gabriel-torres-share-golden-boot |archivedate=August 17, 2016 |accessdate=June 21, 2016 }}</ref> Donovan retired from international soccer on October 10, 2014, having not participated in the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Keh |first=Andrew |date=October 10, 2014 |title=Proper farewell, despite potential for awkwardness |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/10/11/sports/soccer/landon-donovan-is-given-a-proper-farewell-despite-the-potential-for-awkwardness.html?_r=0 |newspaper=The New York Times |accessdate=June 21, 2016}}</ref>

Donovan has scored three [[hat-trick]]s during his international career: against [[Cuba national football team|Cuba]] in the [[2003 CONCACAF Gold Cup]] (where he scored four goals), against [[Ecuador national football team|Ecuador]] in a 2007 friendly, and against [[Scotland national football team|Scotland]] in a 2012 friendly.<ref name="RSSSF"/> The majority of Donovan's goals came in friendlies, with 19 in 63 [[Cap (sport)#Association football|caps]]. He scored most of his competitive goals in the [[CONCACAF Gold Cup]], at 18 in 34 matches, followed by [[FIFA World Cup qualification]] at 13 in 40 matches. Donovan scored five goals in 12 [[FIFA World Cup]] matches at the [[2002 FIFA World Cup|2002]] and 2010 editions. He also scored twice during the [[2009 FIFA Confederations Cup]], including once in the [[2009 FIFA Confederations Cup Final|final]] against [[Brazil national football team|Brazil]].<ref name="RSSSF"/>
