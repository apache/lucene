'''Johann Spenner''' also '''Johann Spender''' (died 5 December 1503) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as [[Bishop of Cologne|Auxiliary Bishop of Cologne]] (1482–1503).<ref name=HierarchiaII-Cyrene />

==Biography==
Johann Spenner was appointed a priest in the [[Order of Friars Minor]].<ref name=CathHierJohSpenn /> On 4 November 1482, he was appointed during the papacy of [[Pope Sixtus IV]] as [[Bishop of Cologne|Auxiliary Bishop of Cologne]] and [[Titular Bishop]] of ''Cyrene''.<ref name=HierarchiaII-Cyrene /><ref name=CathHierJohSpenn /> On 18 November 1482, he was consecrated bishop by [[Stephen Teglatius|Stefan Teglatije]], [[Archbishop of Bar]], with [[Giuliano Maffei]], [[Bishop of Bertinoro]], serving as co-consecrator.<ref name=CathHierJohSpenn /> He served as Auxiliary Bishop of Cologne until his death on 5 December 1503.<ref name=HierarchiaII-Cyrene /><ref name=CathHierJohSpenn />
