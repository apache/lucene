'''Felicity Okpete Ovai''' (born 1961) is an [[engineer]] and [[academic]] from [[Degema, Rivers State]] in [[Nigeria]]. A member of the [[Rivers State People's Democratic Party]], she was the first woman to be appointed [[Rivers State Commissioner of Works|Commissioner of Works]], serving from 2003 to 2006.<ref name="Unity Road">{{cite news|author=Okon Bassey|title=Work Begins On N11.8bn Road Project|url=http://allafrica.com/stories/200504041179.html|accessdate=19 June 2016|work=[[Thisday]]|via=[[AllAfrica]]|date=26 April 2005}}</ref><ref name="Women"/>

==Education==
Ovai completed her [[secondary education]] at [[Holy Rosary Secondary School]] (now Holy Rosary College) in [[Port Harcourt]]. She received a [[B.Tech]] in
[[Agricultural Engineering]] from the [[Rivers State University of Science and Technology]] and a Master of Mechanical
Engineering degree from the [[University of Port Harcourt]].<ref name="Women"/>

==Career==
Ovai has held various positions of responsibility in Rivers State. She served as first woman [[Rivers State Commissioner of Works|Commissioner of Works]] in the [[Executive Council of Rivers State]] from 2003 to 2006.<ref name="Women"/> While in office, she oversaw the commencement of a number of road construction projects in the state including work on the road connecting Ogoni, [[Andoni]], and [[Opobo–Nkoro]] generally christened Unity Road which totaled about ₦11.8 billion.<ref name="Unity Road"/>

On 2 December 2006, it was reported that Okpete Ovai alongside Shedrack Akalokwu, a Special Adviser to [[Governor of Rivers State|Governor]] [[Peter Odili]] and [[Wilson Ake]], a member of [[House of Representatives (Nigeria)|House of the Representatives]] had been cleared by the [[Rivers State People's Democratic Party|People's Democratic Party]] to participate in its [[Rivers West]] [[senatorial]] [[primary election]].<ref>{{cite news|author=|title=PDP Senate Race: Who wins, who loses?|url=http://www.thenationonlineng.net/archive2/tblnews_Detail.php?id=4888|accessdate=17 June 2016|work=[[The Nation (Nigeria)|The Nation]]|date=2 December 2006}}</ref>

In February 2016, Ovai was appointed a member of the [[Rivers State Roads Maintenance and Rehabilitation Agency]] by Governor [[Ezenwo Nyesom Wike]].<ref>{{cite news|author=|title=Wike Orders Tax Authorities To Block Leakages|url=http://www.thetidenewsonline.com/2016/02/10/wike-orders-tax-authorities-to-block-leakages/|accessdate=19 June 2016|work=[[The Tide (Nigeria)|The Tide]]|date=10 February 2016}}</ref> She is also a member of various professional bodies, such as [[Nigerian Society of Engineers|Nigeria Society of Engineers]], [[Institution of Mechanical Engineers|Institute of Mechanical Engineers]] and [[Society of Agricultural Engineers.]]<ref name="Women">{{cite news|author=Juliet Chinda Njiowhor|title=Women On The Rivers Project At 49|url=http://www.thetidenewsonline.com/2016/05/27/women-on-the-rivers-project-at-49/|accessdate=19 June 2016|work=[[The Tide (Nigeria)|The Tide]]|date=27 May 2016}}</ref>
