'''''Biochemistry''''' is a common university textbook used for teaching of [[biochemistry]]. It was initially written by [[Lubert Stryer]] and published by [[W. H. Freeman]] in 1975.<ref name="Dagley">{{cite journal|last1=Dagley|first1=Stanley|title=Biochemistry (book review)|journal=Biochemical Education|date=July 1975|volume=3|issue=3|pages=44–46|doi=10.1016/0307-4412(75)90047-3|url=}}</ref><ref name="King Clinical Chemistry">{{cite journal|last1=King|first1=Stanton L.|title=Biochemistry (book review)|journal=Clinical Chemistry|date=1975|volume=21|issue=9|page=1349|url=http://www.clinchem.org/content/21/9/1349.1.short|accessdate=19 June 2016}}</ref><ref name="Reid 1976">{{cite journal|last1=Reid|first1=R. A.|title=Biochemistry by Lubert Stryer|journal=The Quarterly Review of Biology|date=September 1976|volume=51|issue=3|pages=426|doi=10.1086/409489}}</ref> It has been published in regular editions since.<ref name="Wilson 1988">{{cite journal|last1=Wilson|first1=Jerry L.|title=Biochemistry; Third edition (Stryer, Lubert)|journal=Journal of Chemical Education|date=December 1988|volume=65|issue=12|pages=A337|doi=10.1021/ed065pA337|doi-access=free}}</ref><ref name="Bad Science">{{cite book|last1=Goldacre|first1=Ben|author-link=Ben Goldacre|title=Bad Science|date=2009|publisher=Fourth Estate|location=London|isbn=9780007284870|edition=Fourth Estate pbk.}}</ref><ref name="New Scientist 1988">{{cite journal|last1=Cotterill|first1=Sue|last2=Lichtenstein|first2=Conrad|title=The Biotechnology Boom (book reviews)|journal=[[New Scientist]]|date=April 1988|page=81|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=GDEnVIFJpt4C&pg=PA81|accessdate=19 June 2016}}</ref> It is commonly used as an undergraduate teaching textbook or reference work.<ref name="Mooney-McAuley">{{cite web|last1=Mooney-McAuley|first1=Karen|title=Biochemistry: what it is, what it is not and why it is important|url=https://blogs.qub.ac.uk/qubio/2015/09/23/biochemistry-what-it-is-what-it-is-not-and-why-it-is-important/|publisher=[[Queen's University, Belfast]]|accessdate=19 June 2016|date=2015-09-23}}</ref><ref name="Gray">{{cite journal|last1=Gray|first1=B.|title=Biochemistry|edition=Second|journal=Biochemical Education|date=July 1981|volume=9|issue=3|page=111|doi=10.1016/0307-4412(81)90224-7|url=http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/store/10.1016/0307-4412(81)90224-7/asset/5690090319_ftp.pdf?v=1&t=ipmke6eo&s=da47dde797aa0ab765e0661602a50af2007420ac|accessdate=19 June 2016}}</ref>

More recent editions have been co-written by [[Jeremy M. Berg|Jeremy Berg]], John L. Tymoczko and Gregory J. Gatto Jr and published by [[Palgrave Macmillan]].<ref name="Olivera 2003">{{cite journal|last1=Olivera|first1=B. M.|title=Promising New Directions in Biochemistry|journal=Cell Biology Education|date=1 September 2003|volume=2|issue=3|pages=148–149|doi=10.1187/cbe.03-04-0020|pmc=192447}}</ref> As of 2019, the book has been published in 9 editions.<ref name="Biochemistry 9">{{cite book|last1=Stryer|title=Biochemistry|date=2019|publisher=Palgrave Macmillan|isbn=978-1319114657|edition=9th|display-authors=etal}}</ref> Macmillan have also published additional teaching supplements such as course materials based on the book.<ref name="Student Companion to Accompany Biochemistry (review)">{{cite journal|last1=Wolfson|first1=Adele|title=Student Companion to Accompany Biochemistry (review)|journal=Biochemistry and Molecular Biology Education|date=May 2003|volume=31|issue=3|page=218|doi=10.1002/bmb.2003.494031039993}}</ref><ref name="Student's Companion to Stryer's Biochemistry 1990">{{cite book|last1=Gumport|first1=Richard|last2=Koeppe|first2=Roger|last3=Stryer|first3=Lubert|title=Student's Companion to Stryer's Biochemistry|date=1990|publisher=W.H. Freeman|location=New York|isbn=9780716720751}}</ref><ref name="Lecture Notebook for Biochemistry 2007">{{cite book|last1=Berg|first1=Jeremy|last2=Tymoczko|first2=John|last3=Stryer|first3=Lubert|title=Lecture Notebook for Biochemistry|date=2007|publisher=W.H. Freeman and Co.|location=New York|isbn=978-0716771579|edition=6th ed., 1st print.}}</ref>
