'''Matthew "Matt" Halischuk''' (born June 1, 1988) is a Canadian former professional [[ice hockey]] [[Winger (ice hockey)|right winger]]. He played in the [[National Hockey League]] (NHL) with the [[New Jersey Devils]], [[Nashville Predators]] and [[Winnipeg Jets]]. Halischuk is of [[Ukrainians|Ukrainian]] descent.

==Playing career==
Halischuk was drafted 117th overall in the [[2007 NHL Entry Draft]] by the [[New Jersey Devils]]. He was drafted from the [[Ontario Hockey League]] (OHL) where he played for the [[Kitchener Rangers]] and the [[Toronto St. Michael's Majors]]. He was selected to [[Canada men's national junior ice hockey team|Team Canada]] for the [[2008 IIHF World U20 Championship]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hockeycanada.ca/index.cfm/ci_id/51949/la_id/1/season_id/207/team_id/647/ss_id/21712.htm |title=Canada 2008 World Championship roster | publisher =hockeycanada.com | date = 2008-01-01 | accessdate = 2008-11-10}}</ref> At the Under-20 Championship, he scored the [[game-winning goal]] in [[Overtime (ice hockey)|overtime]] of the [[gold medal]] game.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hockeycanada.ca/index.php/ci_id/11728/la_id/1/ss_id/21712/game_id/1232.htm?|title=Halischuk's overtime goal gives Canada gold medal at World Juniors | publisher =hockeycanada.com | date = 2008-01-14 | accessdate = 2008-11-10}}</ref>

Halischuk made his professional debut in the [[2008–09 AHL season|2008–09]] season with the [[Lowell Devils]], the [[American Hockey League]] (AHL) affiliate of the New Jersey Devils. He played his first NHL game against the [[Toronto Maple Leafs]] on October 29, 2008, at the [[Prudential Center]] and recorded an [[assist (ice hockey)|assist]] before returning to Lowell for the remainder of the year.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.cbssports.com/nhl/gamecenter/recap/NHL_20081029_TOR@NJ?tag=pageRow;pageContainer |title= In seesaw game, Maple Leafs top Devils on Hagman's shootout tally | publisher =[[CBS Sports]] | date = 2008-10-29 | accessdate = 2010-06-20}}</ref> In the following season, [[2009–10 NHL season|2009–10]], Halischuk scored his first NHL goal, against [[José Théodore]] in a 5–2 victory over the [[Washington Capitals]] on November 14, 2009.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.cbssports.com/nhl/gamecenter/recap/NHL_20091114_WAS@NJ?tag=pageRow;pageContainer|title=Devils tally five straight to beat Caps; win 1,000th game in team history | publisher =[[CBS Sports]] | date = 2008-11-14 | accessdate = 2010-06-20}}</ref>

On June 19, 2010, Halischuk was traded by the Devils (along with a second round draft pick) to the [[Nashville Predators]] in exchange for Predators' captain [[Jason Arnott]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.nhl.com/ice/news.htm?id=532100|title=Predators trade Arnott back to Devils | website =[[National Hockey League|NHL]] | date = 2010-06-19 | accessdate = 2010-06-19}}</ref> On April 30, 2011, Halischuk scored the game-winning goal in the second overtime of Game 2 of the Western Conference Semifinal series against the [[Vancouver Canucks]] to even the series at 1–1. It was Nashville's first ever win in the second round of the playoffs.

On July 7, 2011, Halischuk signed a two-year contract with the Predators.<ref>{{Cite web| url = http://prohockeytalk.nbcsports.com/2011/07/07/predators-continue-to-work-through-rfa-offer-sheet-mess-sign-four-players/ | title = Predators continue to work through RFA offer sheet mess, sign four players | publisher = [[NBC Sports]] | date = 2011-07-07 | accessdate = 2011-07-07}}</ref> After the [[2012–13 NHL lockout|lockout]]-shortened [[2012–13 NHL season|2012–13 season]], Halischuk was not given a qualifying offer by the Predators, releasing him as a free agent. On July 11, 2013, he signed a one-year contract with the [[Winnipeg Jets]].<ref>{{cite web| url = https://www.tsn.ca/nhl/story/?id=427514 | title = Jets sign free agent forward Halischuk | publisher = [[The Sports Network]] | date = 2013-07-11 | accessdate = 2013-07-11}}</ref> On July 1, 2015, Halischuk resigned with the Jets with a one-year, $750,000 contract.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cbc.ca/sports/hockey/nhl/jets-retain-adam-pardy-matt-halischuk-bring-back-alex-burmistrov-1.3134673|title=Jets retain Adam Pardy, Matt Halischuk, bring back Alex Burmistrov|publisher=CBC Sports|date=July 1, 2015}}</ref> On December 17, 2015 Halischuk was recalled to the Winnipeg Jets.

On October 29, 2016, Halischuk signed a contract with the [[Iserlohn Roosters]] of the [[Deutsche Eishockey Liga]] (DEL), Germany's top league.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://iserlohn-roosters.de/2016/10/29/roosters-ergaenzen-kader-fuer-aktuelle-saison/|title=Iserlohn Roosters|last=gmbh|first=aliaz werbeagentur|access-date=2016-10-29}}</ref> He made 23 appearances for the Roosters, tallying four goals and six assists, before parting ways with the team on January 23, 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://iserlohn-roosters.de/2017/01/23/matt-halischuk-verlaesst-iserlohn-roosters/|title=Iserlohn Roosters|last=gmbh|first=aliaz werbeagentur|access-date=2017-01-23}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Halischuk is married to Erin Pryde of London, Ontario. Their marriage took place on July 12, 2014.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.erinandmatt2014.com/view/login.aspx?uid=7219146569836125&pId=38694782 |title=Our Wedding |publisher=The Knot |accessdate=2013-11-06 }}{{dead link|date=January 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>
