'''J. B. White''' was a department store chain in the [[Southeastern United States]] founded in [[Augusta, Georgia]] in 1874 by James Brice White, an Irish immigrant.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.vergelive.com/stories/experience-more/past-times/ |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2008-12-17 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090106200802/http://www.vergelive.com/stories/experience-more/past-times/ |archivedate=2009-01-06 }}</ref> In the early 1910s, White sold the store to the H.B. Calvin Company, owner of [[Lord & Taylor]].<ref>{{Cite book|title = Legendary Locals of Augusta|url = https://books.google.com/books?id=P93lAgAAQBAJ|publisher = Arcadia Publishing|date = 2014-03-03|isbn = 9781467101264|language = en|first = Don|last = Rhodes}}</ref> The store's initial offerings included clothing, furniture, appliances and community programs.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title = Slideshow: J.B. White's on Broad|url = http://chronicle.augusta.com/slideshow/slideshow-old-s-jb-whites|website = chronicle.augusta.com|access-date = 2016-01-16}}</ref> 

Owned by now-defunct [[Mercantile Stores]] for most of its existence, most locations of the chain were in [[South Carolina]], though locations existed in Augusta and [[Savannah, Georgia|Savannah]]. Most locations became [[Dillard's]] when the Mercantile Stores chain was sold in 1998 for $2.9 billion; some J. B. White locations in overlapping areas became [[Belk]]. In Augusta, the original downtown store added two [[suburb]]an branches, a full-line store at the National Hill's shopping center and a homegoods only location in the Daniel Village shopping center. Then in 1978, the store left its longtime downtown flagship for a new one at [[Regency Mall (Augusta, Georgia)|Regency Mall]], then twenty years later J.B. White moved to its last flagship location at [[Augusta Mall]] in 1998. shortly before the sale to [[Dillard's]].  The Savannah location opened at [[Savannah Mall]] in 1990.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|title = JB White to lose name {{!}} chronicle.augusta.com|url = http://chronicle.augusta.com/stories/1998/08/27/bus_237132.shtml#.Vpmek5MrLMU|website = chronicle.augusta.com|access-date = 2016-01-16}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title = Herald-Journal - Google News Archive Search|url = https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1876&dat=19950426&id=TdUpAAAAIBAJ&pg=4951,2191108&hl=en|website = news.google.com|access-date = 2016-01-16}}</ref> 

The defunct [[Augusta, Georgia]] store on Broad Street was purchased in June 2007 to be converted into [[condominium]]s.
