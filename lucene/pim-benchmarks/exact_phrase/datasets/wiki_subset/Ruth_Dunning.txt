'''Ruth Dunning''' (17 May 1911 – 27 February 1983) was a Welsh actress of stage, television, and film.

== Personal life ==
Mary Ruth Dunning was born in [[Prestatyn]], [[Denbighshire]], in 1911 (some sources give 1909).<ref name="bfi">{{cite web|url=https://www.bfi.org.uk/films-tv-people/4ce2b9f1f075d|title=Ruth Dunning|website=BFI}}</ref>

== Career ==

=== Stage ===
As a young actress, Dunning was a member of an amateur theatre company in [[Altrincham]]. In 1934, she took over a part from [[Wendy Hiller]] in ''[[Love on the Dole]]'', at the [[Garrick Theatre]] in London.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Hopkins|first=Chris|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=lNB0DwAAQBAJ&newbks=1&newbks_redir=0&lpg=PA222&dq=Ruth%20Dunning%20actress&pg=PA222#v=onepage&q=Ruth%20Dunning%20actress&f=false|title=Walter Greenwood's 'Love on the Dole': Novel, Play, Film|date=2018-11-01|publisher=Oxford University Press|year=|isbn=978-1-78694-869-4|location=|pages=222, note 96|language=en}}</ref> Other stage appearances for Dunning included [[Val Gielgud]]'s ''Punch and Judy'' (1937),<ref>{{Cite news|last=Brown|first=Ivor|date=1937-10-24|title=Vaudeville: 'Punch and Judy'|pages=17|work=The Observer|url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/52031804/vaudeville-punch-and-judyivor-brown/|url-status=live|access-date=2020-05-24|via=Newspapers.com}}</ref> [[A. A. Milne]]'s ''Gentleman Unknown'' (1938),<ref>{{Cite news|last=Brown|first=Ivor|date=1938-11-20|title=St. James' 'Gentleman Unknown'|pages=15|work=The Observer|url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/52033268/st-james-gentleman-unknownivor/|url-status=live|access-date=2020-05-24|via=Newspapers.com}}</ref> [[Ted Willis, Baron Willis|Ted Willis]]' ''The Eyes of Youth'' (1959),<ref>{{Cite news|last=|first=|date=1959-10-20|title=Well-engineered Sentimentality|pages=7|work=The Guardian|url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/52031530/well-engineered-sentimentality/|url-status=live|access-date=2020-05-24|via=Newspapers.com}}</ref> and Willis' adaptation of [[Maxim Gorky|Gorky]]'s ''[[Mother (Gorky novel)|Mother]]'' (1961).<ref>{{Cite news|last=R.B.M.|first=|date=1961-05-17|title='Mother'|pages=7|work=The Guardian|url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/52032088/motherrbm/|url-status=live|access-date=2020-05-24|via=Newspapers.com}}</ref>

=== Film and television ===
Dunning found fame in the role of Gladys Grove in [[BBC Television]]'s ''[[The Grove Family]]'' (1954–1957), also portraying that character in the 1955 film ''[[It's a Great Day]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.screenonline.org.uk/tv/id/518438/index.html|title=BFI Screenonline: Grove Family, The (1954-57)|website=www.screenonline.org.uk}}</ref> In 1956, she appeared in a television commercial for [[Persil]] laundry detergent, on the first night of [[ITV Granada|Granada Television]]'s broadcasts in the north of England.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Nixon|first=Sean|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=3m-5DwAAQBAJ&lpg=PT154&ots=lQoliIoUUl&dq=Ruth%20Dunning%20actress&pg=PT154#v=onepage&q=Ruth%20Dunning%20actress&f=false|title=Hard sell: Advertising, affluence and transatlantic relations, c. 1951–69|date=2016-05-16|publisher=Manchester University Press|isbn=978-1-5261-1116-6|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|last=Dickason|first=Renée|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Fyy5aMpa468C&lpg=PA39&ots=VVxFmUrPW2&dq=Ruth%20Dunning%20actress&pg=PA39#v=onepage&q=Ruth%20Dunning%20actress&f=false|title=British Television Advertising: Cultural Identity and Communication|date=2000|publisher=Indiana University Press|year=|isbn=978-1-86020-571-2|location=|pages=39|language=en}}</ref> In 1962 she was awarded the [[British Academy Television Award for Best Actress]] for her work on ''[[Armchair Theatre]]''.<ref>[http://awards.bafta.org/award/1961/television/actress "Television: Actress in 1961"] BAFTA.</ref>

Other screen roles played by Dunning included Leonie in ''[[Intimate Relations (1953 film)|Intimate Relations]]'' (1953), Auntie B. in ''[[Urge to Kill (film)|Urge to Kill]]'' (1960), Mrs. Mitchell in ''[[Hoffman (film)|Hoffman]]'' (1970), Betty Atherton in ''[[The Sextet]]'' (1972), Agnes Henderson in ''[[The House in Nightmare Park]]'' (1973), Miss Minchin in ''[[A Little Princess (1973 TV serial)|A Little Princess]]'' (1973), Mildred Finch in ''[[An Unofficial Rose]]'' (1974–1975), [[Lesley Whittle]]'s mother in ''[[The Black Panther (1977 film)|The Black Panther]]'' (1977), and Mrs. Crabtree in ''[[Children of the Stones]]'' (1977).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmovie.com/artist/ruth-dunning-p128370|title=Ruth Dunning - Movies and Filmography|website=AllMovie}}</ref><ref name="bfi" />

== Personal life ==
Ruth Dunning was married to the actor [[Jack Allen (actor)|Jack Allen]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Obituary: Jack Allen|url=http://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/obituary-jack-allen-1585281.html|date=1995-06-07|website=The Independent|language=en|access-date=2020-05-24}}</ref> She died in 1983, aged 71 years, in London. The Ruth Dunning and Jack Allen Collection at the [[University of Bristol]] holds some of her papers, including contracts, scripts, and photographs.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ruth Dunning and Jack Allen Collection|url=http://www.calmview2.eu/BristolTheatreArchive/CalmView/Record.aspx?src=CalmView.Catalog&id=BTC67|last=|first=|date=|website=Bristol Theatre Archive|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref>
