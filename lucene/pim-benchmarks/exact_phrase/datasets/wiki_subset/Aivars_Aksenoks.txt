'''Aivars Aksenoks''' (born May 24, 1961) is a Latvian politician. A founder of the right-wing [[New Era Party|New Era]], Aksenoks has a degree in engineering from the [[Riga Technical University]] and was the director of State Road and Traffic Safety Agency from 1992–2002. He was also the Minister of Justice, serving from 2003–2004.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://latvia.sitegen.no/customers/latvia/index.php?articleid=213&expand=1&PHPSESSID=7c01c5f11a1717417f5a3b8fb8054898 |title=The Baltic Times: The quiet man behind the wild city |access-date=2007-12-09 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070929024258/http://latvia.sitegen.no/customers/latvia/index.php?articleid=213&expand=1&PHPSESSID=7c01c5f11a1717417f5a3b8fb8054898 |archive-date=2007-09-29 |url-status=dead }}</ref> However, he is best known for being the mayor of [[Riga]] from March 29, 2005 to February 19, 2007.

He moved from [[Jaunais Laiks|JL]] to [[For Fatherland and Freedom/LNNK]] in 2008. Since 2009 Aksenoks is director of the [[Riga Motor Museum]].
