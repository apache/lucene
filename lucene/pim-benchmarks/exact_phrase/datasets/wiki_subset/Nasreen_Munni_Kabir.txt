''' Nasreen Munni Kabir''' (born 1950)<ref name=exp/> is an [[India]]-born television producer, director and author based in the U.K.<ref>{{cite web | title = Noteworthy! |publisher= The Hindu | url = http://www.thehindu.com/todays-paper/tp-features/tp-cinemaplus/noteworthy/article1762552.ece |date=24 April 2011| accessdate = 29 March 2014 | first = Anuj | last = Kumar  }}</ref> She is best known for producing an annual season of Indian films for the British terrestrial television channel [[Channel 4]].

Her work includes the 46-part series ''[[Movie Mahal]]'', ''[[In Search of Guru Dutt]]'', ''Follow that Star'' (a profile of [[Amitabh Bachchan]]), and [[Channel 4]] series such as ''[[How To Make It Big in Bollywood]]'' & ''The Inner and Outer World of Shah Rukh Khan''.  She won the 1999 Women of Achievement Award in Arts & Culture in the UK and became a governor on the board of the [[British Film Institute]] in 2000, serving a six-year term.

Over the years, she has directed several documentaries and published 15 books on cinema, including five books featuring the dialogue of Indian celebrated classic films and book-length biographical conversations with personalities in the [[Hindi film industry]], [[Javed Akhtar]], [[Lata Mangeshkar]], [[A.R. Rahman]], [[Gulzar]], and [[Waheeda Rehman]].<ref name=exp/><ref name=mint/> Her latest book is a biography of legendary tabla virtuoso [[Zakir Hussain (musician)|Ustad Zakir Hussain]]<ref>{{Cite web|last=Kabir|first=Nasreen Munni|title=The Zakir Hussain interview: ‘What do I bring to the tabla? I think it is openness and clarity’|url=https://scroll.in/magazine/865112/zakir-hussain-on-how-the-band-shakti-paved-the-way-for-world-music|access-date=2020-11-18|website=Scroll.in|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Zakir Hussain|url=https://www.goodreads.com/work/best_book/59642388-zakir-hussain-a-life-in-music|access-date=2020-11-18|website=www.goodreads.com}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Born in [[Hyderabad, India]], Kabir's parents moved to London when she was age three.  She did her master's in cinema studies.<ref name=exp>{{cite web | title = Coffee with kabir |publisher= The Indian Express| url = http://indianexpress.com/article/entertainment/entertainment-others/coffee-with-kabir/ |pages=1–3|date=30 March 2014|accessdate = 30 March 2014}}</ref>

==Career==
Kabir moved to Paris and lived there for 19 years, studying film and working as an assistant on various documentaries. She also worked with French film director [[Robert Bresson]] as his trainee assistant on the film FOUR NIGHTS OF A DREAMER. She also worked as a consultant with [[Pompidou Centre]] in Paris, where she organised two Indian film festivals in 1983/85. In 1982, she resettled in London where she began her current job as Channel 4 TV's consultant on Indian Films. In 1986, she directed and produced a 46-part television docu-series on [[Indian cinema]] titled ''Movie Mahal'' for [[Channel 4 TV UK]].<ref name=exp/> and other series on Hindi cinema.

In 2005, she produced a two-part documentary on [[Shah Rukh Khan|Shahrukh Khan]], ''[[The Inner and Outer World of Shah Rukh Khan]]''. It featured the superstar's 2004 Temptations concert tour, the film contrasts Khan's "inner world" of family and daily life, with the "outer world" of his work. She continues to curate an annual season of Indian films for Channel 4 each year. She has also directed a six-part series for Channel 4 UK on the legendary playback singer Lata Mangeshkar called LATA IN HER OWN VOICE.

In April 2011, a book titled "A. R. Rahman The Spirit of Music" written based on the conversations with Nasreen Munni Kabir was released. Also in 2011, she produced ''Bismillah of Benares'' a documentary on shehnai maestro, [[Bismillah Khan]]. It was presented by A.R. Rahman's KM Musiq and distributed by Sony Music in India.<ref>{{cite web | title = 'I wish he had lived to see it' |publisher= The Hindu | url = http://www.thehindu.com/features/magazine/i-wish-he-had-lived-to-see-it/article2573386.ece |date=29 October 2011| accessdate = 29 March 2014 }}</ref>

April 2014 saw the release of a book named 'Conversations with Waheeda Rehman' which was based on her conversations with [[Waheeda Rehman]] about the latter's life and work.<ref name=mint>{{cite web | title = Nasreen Munni Kabir: My biggest problem is, who next? |publisher= Livemint | url = http://www.livemint.com/Leisure/vszsnH7XCQCnC8PoE2oSoN/Nasreen-Munni-Kabir--My-biggest-problem-is-who-next.html |date=29 March 2014| accessdate = 29 March 2014 }}</ref><ref>http://www.penguinbooksindia.com/en/content/conversations-waheeda-rehman</ref>
