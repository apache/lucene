"'''The Door Is Still Open to My Heart'''" is a 1955 song written by [[Chuck Willis]] and originally performed by the Baltimore-based R&B vocal group, [[The Cardinals]]. In the US, the original version peaked at number four on the R&B playlist and number ten in R&B sales charts.<ref>{{cite book |title= Top R&B/Hip-Hop Singles: 1942-2004|last=Whitburn |first=Joel |authorlink=Joel Whitburn |year=2004 |publisher=Record Research |page=102}}</ref> Later in 1955, [[Don Cornell]] recorded the song and released it as the B-side to his hit, "Most of All". [[The Hilltoppers (band)|The Hilltoppers]] had a moderate hit with this song the same year.

==Dean Martin version==
In 1964, [[Dean Martin]] recorded the most successful version of the song. Martin's version spent 11 weeks on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart, peaking at No. 6,<ref>[http://www.billboard.com/artist/300598/dean-martin/chart?f=379 Dean Martin - Chart History - The Hot 100], ''Billboard.com''.
