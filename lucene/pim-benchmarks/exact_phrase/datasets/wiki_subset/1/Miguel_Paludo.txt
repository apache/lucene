'''Miguel Paludo''' (born July 26, 1983 in [[Nova Prata]], [[Rio Grande do Sul]], [[Brazil]]) is a Brazilian professional [[racing driver]]. A Five-time champion in [[Porsche GT3 Cup Brasil]] competition, he formerly competed in the [[NASCAR]] [[Camping World Truck Series]].

==Early career==
Paludo began racing [[go kart]]s at the age of 14, and in 2004, moved up to [[touring car racing]] in a regional Brazilian series, competing for his family-owned team with his brother Daniel.<ref name="Bio">{{cite web|url=http://www.miguelpaludo.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=66&Itemid=101|title=Biography|publisher=Miguel Paludo official site|accessdate=2012-01-13}}</ref> He competed for several years in regional and national series in Brazil, most successfully in the [[Porsche GT3 Cup Brasil]], where he won back-to-back series championships in 2008 and 2009.<ref name="Bio"/><ref name="Bristol">{{cite web|url=http://nascar.speedtv.com/article/trucks-brazils-miguel-paludo-nascar-debut-set-for-bristol-motor-speedway/|title=Brazil’s Miguel Paludo Debut Set For Bristol|date=August 17, 2010|work=SPEED Channel|publisher=Fox Sports|accessdate=2012-01-13|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100817230108/http://nascar.speedtv.com/article/trucks-brazils-miguel-paludo-nascar-debut-set-for-bristol-motor-speedway/|archivedate=August 17, 2010}}</ref>

==NASCAR career==
[[File:Miguel Paludo Turner Motorsports Chevrolet Rockingham 2012.jpg|thumb|left|Paludo at [[Rockingham Speedway|Rockingham]] in 2012.]]
[[File:Texas Pit Stop.jpg|thumb|Paludo making a pit stop at [[Texas Motor Speedway|Texas]] in 2012.]]
Paludo began his NASCAR career in 2010, competing in the [[K&N Pro Series East]] in a car fielded by [[Hattori Racing Enterprises]]. He finished fifteenth in the series standings that year, with a best finish of fourth.<ref name="Bio"/> He also made his debut in the [[Camping World Truck Series]], competing for [[Germain Racing]] and [[Red Horse Racing]] in four races.<ref name="Bio"/> He finished ninth in his first race in the series, at [[Bristol Motor Speedway]], widely regarded as a difficult track for rookie drivers.<ref name="Bristol2"/>

In 2011, he ran full-time in the Camping World Truck Series for [[Red Horse Racing]], with a best finish of third;<ref name="Bristol2">{{cite web|url=http://nascar.speedtv.com/article/trucks-miguel-paludo-progressing-pumped-for-bristol-motor-speedway-return/|title=Paludo Progressing, Pumped For Bristol Return|last=Turner|first=Jared|date=August 23, 2011|work=SPEED Channel|publisher=Fox Sports|accessdate=2012-01-13|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://archive.is/20130202223529/http://nascar.speedtv.com/article/trucks-miguel-paludo-progressing-pumped-for-bristol-motor-speedway-return/|archivedate=February 2, 2013}}</ref> he ended the season finishing seventeenth in the points standings and fourth in the series' [[NASCAR Rookie of the Year|Rookie-of-the-Year]] competition.<ref name="TS"/>

Following the end of the 2011 season, Paludo left Red Horse Racing to drive for [[Turner Scott Motorsports]] in the No. 32 truck with teammates [[James Buescher]] and countryman [[Nelson Piquet, Jr.]].<ref name="TS">{{cite web|url=http://www.scenedaily.com/news/articles/truckseries/Turner_Motorsports_adds_Brazilians_Nelson_Piquet_Jr_Miguel_Paludo_to_2012_truck_lineup.html|title=Turner Motorsports adds Brazilians Nelson Piquet Jr., Miguel Paludo to 2012 truck lineup|last=Pockrass|first=Bob|date=November 29, 2011|work=SceneDaily.com|publisher=The Sporting News|accessdate=2012-01-13}}</ref> Paludo started off the season with a pole in the season-opener at [[Daytona International Speedway]], where he was the first Brazilian to win a pole in one of NASCAR's top-three series, and alongside Piquet Jr., comprised the first all-Brazilian front row in the history of NASCAR. Paludo's best finish of the season (fifth) came in the season finale at [[Homestead-Miami Speedway]], and he ultimately finished 10th in the NCWTS driver point standings, his best points position to date.
Additionally, Paludo ran two [[NASCAR Nationwide Series]] races in 2012, competing at [[Road America]] and [[Watkins Glen International]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cbssports.com/nascar/blog/eye-on-nascar/19216420/four-nationwide-entries-for-turner-motorsports-at-road-america|title=Miguel Paludo and Nelson Piquet Jr. join Turner Road America Nationwide plan|last=Pistone|first=Pete|date=May 31, 2012|work=Eye on NASCAR|publisher=CBS Sports|accessdate=2012-05-31}}</ref> in the latter of which he earned his best NNS finish of 13th.

In 2013, Paludo returned to the No. 32 Chevrolet Silverado with Turner Scott Motorsports for another complete NCWTS season.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sbnation.com/nascar/2012/12/3/3724338/miguel-paludo-nascar-truck-series-2013|title=NASCAR Trucks driver Miguel Paludo returning to Turner Motorsports in 2013}}</ref> At Talladega Superspeedway in October, Paludo was involved in a spectacular last-lap accident, his truck being flipped in the [[trioval]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/nascar/2013/10/19/kyle-busch-darrell-wallace-trucks-talladega/3069845/|title=Justin Lofton taken to hospital after Truck crash at Talladega|last=Ryan|first=Nate|date=October 19, 2013|work=[[USA Today]]|accessdate=2013-10-19|location=McLean, VA}}</ref>

Following the 2013 season, Paludo was released by Turner Scott Motorsports, due to a lack of sponsorship.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://popularspeed.com/paludo-its-hard-to-believe-im-not-racing-this-year/|title=Paludo: ‘It’s Hard to Believe I’m Not Racing This Year’|last=Crandall|first=Kelly|date=January 31, 2014|work=PopularSpeed|accessdate=2014-03-05}}</ref>

==Personal life==
[[File:Number 32 truck at TMS.jpg|thumb|right|Paludo's No. 32 Unite for Diabetes/Duroline Chevrolet paint scheme at Texas Motor Speedway]]
Born in [[Nova Prata]] in the state of [[Rio Grande do Sul]] in southern Brazil,<ref name="Bio"/> Paludo currently resides in [[Mooresville, North Carolina]].<ref name="iG">{{cite web|url=http://grandepremio.ig.com.br/outrascategorias/turismo/2011/12/27/stop+amp+go+miguel+paludo+10541777.html|title=Stop & Go: Miguel Paludo|last=Silva|first=Fernando|date=December 27, 2011|work=Grande Prêmio|publisher=iG Esporte|accessdate=2012-01-14}}</ref> He is married, and has one son, Oliver, with his wife Patricia.<ref name="Bristol2"/>

===Diabetes advocacy===
Paludo was diagnosed with [[Diabetes mellitus type 1|type 1 diabetes]] in 2004 at the age of 21 and has since become an advocate of [[diabetes]] awareness and care. In addition to a long-standing relationship with the [[American Diabetes Association]], Paludo announced in 2012 that he would become the next Blue Circle Champion for the [[International Diabetes Federation]], a worldwide alliance of some 200 diabetes associations in more than 160 countries. Paludo is the first representative of the organization from [[South America]].<ref name="PaludoDia">{{cite web|url=http://turnermotorsportsllc.com/news/show/51016-miguel-paludo-partners-with-international-diabetes-federation/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140319000510/http://turnermotorsportsllc.com/news/show/51016-miguel-paludo-partners-with-international-diabetes-federation/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=March 19, 2014 |title=Miguel Paludo Partners With International Diabetes Federation |date=May 30, 2012 |publisher=Turner Motorsports |accessdate=2012-06-11 }}</ref>

Paludo and his wife Patricia also learned in 2012 that their son Oliver, at the time only eight months old, was also a type 1 diabetic. In response to the news, Paludo ran a special No. 32 Unite for Diabetes/Duroline paint scheme in the June 2012 NCWTS race at [[Texas Motor Speedway]]. The scheme featured the blue circle, which is the international symbol for diabetes, and an image of Paludo and his son on the truck's [[tonneau]] cover. In November of that year, Paludo ran a [[World Diabetes Day]] paint scheme at both Texas Motor Speedway and [[Phoenix International Raceway]].<ref name="PaludoDia"/>
